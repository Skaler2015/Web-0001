ℹ️ About This Tool

The Employee Provident Fund (EPF) is a mandatory retirement savings scheme in India administered by the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO). Both you and your employer contribute 12% of your basic salary plus Dearness Allowance (DA) every month — your 12% goes entirely into the EPF account, while the employer's 12% is split between 8.33% into the Employee Pension Scheme (EPS) and only 3.67% into the EPF account.

The EPF balance earns compound interest at a rate declared annually by the government — currently 8.15% per annum. Over a working lifetime of 25–35 years, this compounding effect is substantial. This calculator estimates the total EPF corpus you'll accumulate by retirement, factoring in your annual salary increases which increase the monthly contribution amount each year.

How to use:

  1. Enter your Basic Salary plus DA (not the full CTC — typically 40–50% of gross salary).
  2. Enter your current age and expected retirement age.
  3. Enter your expected annual salary increment percentage.
  4. Click "Calculate" to see monthly contributions and projected retirement corpus.

Key EPF rules to know

You can withdraw your PF balance after leaving a job and waiting 2 months, or at retirement. Partial withdrawals are allowed for medical emergencies, house purchase, and education under specific EPFO rules. Interest on PF is tax-free up to 2.5 lakh annual employee contribution (5 lakh for government employees). UAN (Universal Account Number) links all your EPF accounts across employers, ensuring continuity even when you change jobs.

Many people underestimate how significant their EPF corpus grows to be by retirement, especially with regular salary increases. Even at a modest starting salary, a 30-year EPF contribution history compounded at current rates builds a multi-crore corpus that forms the backbone of retirement income for millions of Indian workers. This makes it one of the most valuable, tax-advantaged, low-risk wealth building instruments available to salaried employees in India.

One of the most powerful aspects of EPF is that contributions are mandatory — both employee and employer must contribute every month, which enforces a savings discipline that purely voluntary investment vehicles can't replicate. Over a long career, even modest monthly contributions compound into a significant retirement fund. Starting early amplifies this effect dramatically: an employee starting EPF contributions at 22 will accumulate roughly twice as much at retirement as one who starts at 32, even if the later starter contributes a larger monthly amount due to a higher salary at that point.

जानने लायक EPF के मुख्य नियम

Job छोड़ने के बाद 2 महीने इंतज़ार करने पर, या retirement पर PF balance withdraw कर सकते हैं। Medical emergencies, house purchase, और education के लिए EPFO के specific नियमों के तहत partial withdrawals allowed हैं। PF पर interest ₹2.5 लाख annual employee contribution तक tax-free है (government employees के लिए ₹5 लाख)। UAN (Universal Account Number) सभी employers के सभी EPF accounts को link करता है, job changes के दौरान भी continuity सुनिश्चित करता है।

EPF का सबसे powerful पहलू यह है कि contributions mandatory हैं — employee और employer दोनों को हर महीने contribute करना पड़ता है, जो purely voluntary investment vehicles की तुलना में savings discipline enforce करता है। 22 साल में EPF contributions शुरू करने वाला employee retirement तक लगभग उतना ही accumulate करेगा जितना 32 साल में शुरू करने वाले से दोगुना — भले ही बाद वाले की salary ज़्यादा हो और वह larger amounts contribute करे, क्योंकि compounding समय के साथ exponentially काम करता है।

बहुत से लोग यह underestimate करते हैं कि retirement तक उनका EPF corpus कितना significant बन जाता है, खासकर regular salary increases के साथ। Modest starting salary पर भी, current rates पर compound की गई 30-year EPF contribution history एक multi-crore corpus बनाती है जो लाखों Indian workers की retirement income की backbone बनती है। यह भारत में salaried employees के लिए available सबसे valuable, tax-advantaged, low-risk wealth building instruments में से एक बनाता है।
