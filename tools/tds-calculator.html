📄 Introduction

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is one of the most common — and most frequently miscalculated — compliance tasks for any business, professional, or individual who makes payments in India. Every payer needs to know instantly: is TDS applicable to this payment at all, which section governs it, what rate applies given the payee's PAN status and any certificate they hold, and what the resulting net payment actually is. Doing this by hand across ten different sections, each with its own threshold, rate and PAN/certificate rules, is exactly the kind of repetitive, error-prone calculation a purpose-built tool should handle. The Professional TDS Calculator covers Salary, Contractor, Professional Fees, Commission, Rent, Property Purchase, Interest, Dividend, E-commerce and Virtual Digital Asset payments — instantly, in your browser, with nothing ever uploaded.

✨ Features

Pick a TDS type and the calculator switches its entire rate/threshold engine, showing only the fields relevant to that section. Enter taxpayer details — taxpayer type, residential status, PAN availability, a Section 197 lower/nil deduction certificate, or a Form 15G/15H self-declaration — and the engine applies each override in the correct priority order. Enter payment details — gross amount, whether GST is already included, the payment date, and any payments already made to the same payee earlier in the year — and the applicable threshold, rate, TDS amount and net payable update instantly. A dedicated Salary tab computes annual tax liability from slabs for Section 192. Multiple Payment Mode lets you add unlimited payment rows and see the aggregate threshold crossed and TDS caught up automatically, exactly as it would apply to a recurring payee across a financial year.

🏷️ TDS Types Covered

Ten dedicated sections are built in: 192 (Salary), 194C (Contractor Payments), 194J (Professional/Technical Fees), 194H (Commission/Brokerage), 194I (Rent), 194IA (Property Purchase), 194A (Interest), 194 (Dividend), 194O (E-commerce Participant) and 194S (Virtual Digital Assets/Crypto), plus a flexible Other Payments option with a custom rate and threshold for anything not explicitly listed.

🧠 The TDS Engine

Behind every calculation, the engine resolves the applicable threshold and rate for the selected section and taxpayer context, then applies overrides in a clear priority order: a Form 15G/15H self-declaration (interest and dividend only) zeroes TDS outright, subject to the declarant's own eligibility; a Section 197 lower/nil deduction certificate overrides the standard rate with the certificate rate; otherwise, if the threshold isn't crossed, no TDS applies; and if it is crossed but the payee has no PAN, Section 206AA raises the rate to the higher of 20% or the section's own rate. Every one of these steps, and the resulting rate and amount, is shown transparently in the "TDS Engine Determination" panel — nothing is a black box.

🪪 PAN Rules & Section 206AA

Section 206AA requires a higher TDS rate — the higher of 20% or the section's own rate — whenever the payee hasn't furnished their PAN, once the section's threshold is otherwise crossed. This calculator applies that rule automatically the moment you select "PAN Not Available," and flags it clearly in the Engine Determination panel so you can see exactly why the rate jumped.

📜 Form 16, Form 16A, Form 26AS & TRACES

Form 16 is the annual TDS certificate an employer issues for salary TDS under Section 192, covering the whole financial year. Form 16A is the equivalent certificate for all non-salary TDS — contractor payments, professional fees, rent, interest and the rest — issued quarterly. Form 26AS (now largely superseded in day-to-day use by the Annual Information Statement, but still referenced widely) is the consolidated tax-credit statement showing every TDS/TCS entry against your PAN, and TRACES (TDS Reconciliation Analysis and Correction Enabling System) is the government portal where deductors file TDS returns, generate Form 16/16A, and deductees can view and reconcile their credited TDS.

💸 Refund Process

If the TDS deducted across the year exceeds your actual tax liability, the excess becomes a refund, claimed by filing your income tax return and reconciling it against your Form 26AS/AIS entries — the refund is processed and credited directly to your bank account by the Income Tax Department after the return is verified and processed.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

The most frequent errors this calculator helps avoid: deducting TDS on the GST-inclusive amount instead of the taxable base; missing the aggregate threshold when a payee receives several small payments across the year that individually stay under a single-payment limit; forgetting to apply the Section 206AA higher rate when PAN isn't available; and applying the standard rate despite a valid Section 197 certificate or Form 15G/15H declaration on file.

ℹ️ About This Tool

Tax Deducted at Source, universally known as TDS, is India's mechanism for collecting income tax at the exact moment a payment is made rather than waiting for the recipient to file a return months later. Whoever pays — an employer, a company hiring a contractor, a tenant paying rent above a threshold, a bank crediting interest — is legally required to deduct a specified percentage of the payment and deposit it with the government on the payee's behalf, issuing a certificate (Form 16 or 16A) that the payee later uses to claim credit for that tax when filing their own return. The Professional TDS Calculator is built to answer the question every payer and payee actually has in the moment: for this specific payment, under this specific section, with this specific PAN and certificate situation, exactly how much TDS applies?

The calculator covers ten distinct sections, each governed by its own combination of rate and threshold. Section 192 handles salary, where TDS isn't a flat percentage of the gross payment but rather the employee's estimated annual tax liability — computed from the applicable slab rates under the old or new regime, after standard deduction and any admissible deductions — spread across the year's paychecks. Section 194C covers payments to contractors and sub-contractors, at 1% for individual or HUF contractors and 2% for everyone else, once a single payment exceeds ₹30,000 or the aggregate paid to that contractor during the year exceeds ₹1,00,000. Section 194J covers professional and technical fees, at 10% for genuinely professional services (legal, medical, engineering, accountancy, and similar) but a reduced 2% for technical services and call-centre operations, once the ₹50,000 threshold is crossed. Section 194H covers commission and brokerage at 2% above a ₹20,000 threshold. Section 194I covers rent, split into 10% for land, building and furniture and a reduced 2% for plant, machinery and equipment, once the annual threshold is crossed. Section 194IA applies a flat 1% to the sale consideration whenever an immovable property transaction crosses ₹50 lakh. Section 194A covers interest other than on securities — typically bank fixed deposits — at 10%, with a threshold that depends on whether the payer is a bank or post office (with a higher limit for senior citizens) or another kind of payer entirely. Section 194 covers dividend income at 10% above a modest ₹10,000 threshold. Section 194O covers payments an e-commerce operator makes to a participant seller on its platform, at a low 0.1% rate above a ₹5 lakh threshold. And Section 194S, one of the newer sections, covers the transfer of virtual digital assets — cryptocurrency and similar assets — at 1%, with the threshold depending on whether the payer qualifies as a "specified person" under the Act.

Underneath all ten sections sits a single, consistent engine for resolving the actual rate and threshold that apply to your specific payment. It starts by computing the correct taxable base — excluding the GST component if you've indicated the gross amount includes it, since GST charged separately on an invoice is not part of the sum on which TDS is calculated, per CBDT's own clarification. It then checks the relevant threshold: a straightforward single-payment or annual-aggregate limit for most sections, or the combined single-payment-or-aggregate rule that Section 194C specifically uses. From there, three overrides are applied in a strict, transparent order. A Form 15G/15H self-declaration — available for interest and dividend payments to residents whose total income is expected to remain below the taxable threshold — zeroes the TDS entirely, provided the declarant genuinely meets the eligibility conditions, which remain their own responsibility to confirm. A Section 197 lower or nil deduction certificate, obtained in advance from the Assessing Officer, overrides the standard rate with whatever rate the certificate specifies, whether that's a reduced percentage or zero. And if neither applies, but the payee hasn't furnished their PAN, Section 206AA steps in to raise the effective rate to the higher of 20% or the section's own rate — a rule this calculator applies automatically and flags clearly, since it's one of the most commonly missed compliance points in manual TDS calculations.

For payees who receive several payments from the same payer across a financial year — a retainer paid monthly, quarterly rent, recurring commission — the Multiple Payment Mode models exactly how TDS obligations evolve as the cumulative total crosses a threshold partway through the year. Add as many payment rows as you need, each with its own date and amount, and the calculator tracks the running cumulative total against the threshold, applying TDS from the point of crossing onward and, for the specific payment that triggers the crossing, a one-time catch-up that reflects the tax that should have applied on the amount already paid without deduction. An "auto-generate" helper can build out a full year's worth of rows automatically from the payment amount, frequency and count you specify in the Payment Details tab, saving you from entering each row by hand for a simple recurring schedule.

Every calculation surfaces instantly across a full dashboard: gross amount, taxable amount, the applicable TDS rate, the TDS amount itself, the resulting net payment, and the effective TDS percentage on the gross figure, backed by a calculation waterfall that shows exactly how the final number was reached and two charts — a payment-distribution view and a TDS-breakdown view — that update the moment any input changes. Results export cleanly to PDF or Excel, copy to your clipboard as a plain-text summary, share directly through your device's native share sheet, or print with the site's navigation automatically hidden for a clean physical or PDF copy. A Light/Dark theme toggle and an English/Hindi language toggle for the interface, alongside High Contrast and Large Font accessibility options, make the tool comfortable to use in whichever setting and language suits you. And, as with every tool on this site, all of it runs entirely inside your own browser: no payment figure, PAN status, or certificate detail is ever uploaded anywhere, there's no account or sign-up, and there is no AI-powered functionality anywhere in the calculator — every result is the output of deterministic, auditable, hand-written logic straight from the published provisions of the Income Tax Act.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is TDS?

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is a mechanism where the payer of certain specified payments — salary, contractor fees, rent, interest, commission and more — deducts income tax at a prescribed rate before making the payment, and deposits it with the government on the payee's behalf. / TDS एक व्यवस्था है जिसमें भुगतान करने वाला भुगतान से पहले एक निर्धारित rate पर income tax काट लेता है और सरकार के पास जमा कर देता है।

Why does TDS exist?

It ensures a steady flow of tax revenue throughout the year rather than a lump sum after everyone files their return, and it widens the tax net by capturing income at the source, reducing the chance of it going unreported. / यह पूरे साल स्थिर tax राजस्व सुनिश्चित करता है और आय को स्रोत पर ही पकड़ लेता है।

Which sections does this calculator cover?

Section 192 (Salary), 194C (Contractor), 194J (Professional/Technical Fees), 194H (Commission), 194I (Rent), 194IA (Property Purchase), 194A (Interest), 194 (Dividend), 194O (E-commerce) and 194S (Virtual Digital Assets), plus a customisable "Other Payments" option. / यह calculator दस sections और एक customisable "Other Payments" विकल्प को cover करता है।

How is TDS on salary (Section 192) calculated?

Unlike other sections, salary TDS isn't a flat percentage — it's the employee's estimated annual income tax liability (computed from slab rates under the chosen regime, after standard deduction and eligible deductions), spread proportionately across the year's salary payments. / Salary TDS flat प्रतिशत नहीं बल्कि slab rates से गणना की गई वार्षिक tax liability है, जो साल के भुगतानों में बँटी होती है।

What is the TDS rate and threshold for contractor payments (194C)?

1% for individual/HUF contractors, 2% for others, applicable once a single payment exceeds ₹30,000 or the aggregate paid to that contractor during the financial year exceeds ₹1,00,000. / व्यक्ति/HUF ठेकेदारों के लिए 1%, बाकी के लिए 2%, ₹30,000 (एकल) या ₹1,00,000 (aggregate) की threshold पर।

What is the TDS rate for professional fees (194J)?

10% for professional services (legal, medical, engineering, accountancy and similar) and a reduced 2% for technical services and call-centre operations, once the ₹50,000 annual threshold is crossed. / Professional services के लिए 10%, technical services के लिए 2%, ₹50,000 threshold पर।

What is the TDS rate for commission (194H)?

2%, applicable once payments to the same payee exceed ₹20,000 in the financial year. / ₹20,000 threshold पार करने पर 2%।

What is the TDS rate for rent (194I)?

10% for land, building and furniture; a reduced 2% for plant, machinery and equipment; applicable once the annual threshold of ₹6,00,000 is crossed. / Land/building/furniture के लिए 10%, plant/machinery के लिए 2%, ₹6,00,000 वार्षिक threshold पर।

What is the TDS rate for property purchase (194IA)?

A flat 1% of the sale consideration, applicable whenever the transaction value is ₹50 lakh or more. / बिक्री राशि पर flat 1%, जब लेन-देन ₹50 lakh या अधिक हो।

What is the TDS rate for interest (194A)?

10%, with a threshold of ₹50,000 (₹1,00,000 for senior citizens) when the payer is a bank or post office, or ₹10,000 for other payers such as company deposits. / 10%, bank/post office के लिए ₹50,000 (senior citizen ₹1,00,000), अन्य के लिए ₹10,000।

What is the TDS rate for dividend income?

10%, applicable once dividend paid to a shareholder exceeds ₹10,000 in the financial year. / ₹10,000 threshold पार करने पर 10%।

What is the TDS rate under Section 194O for e-commerce?

0.1% of the gross amount an e-commerce operator pays to a participant seller, applicable once payments exceed ₹5,00,000 in the financial year. / e-commerce operator द्वारा seller को भुगतान पर ₹5,00,000 threshold पर 0.1%।

What is the TDS rate for virtual digital assets / crypto (194S)?

1%, with a threshold of ₹50,000 for a "specified person" or ₹10,000 for others. / "specified person" के लिए ₹50,000, अन्य के लिए ₹10,000 threshold पर 1%।

What happens if the payee doesn't have a PAN?

Section 206AA requires the higher of 20% or the section's own applicable rate to be deducted instead of the standard rate, once the threshold is otherwise crossed. This calculator applies it automatically when you select "PAN Not Available." / PAN न होने पर Section 206AA के तहत 20% या लागू rate में से जो अधिक हो, वह काटा जाता है।

What is a Lower/Nil Deduction Certificate?

Issued under Section 197 by the Assessing Officer, it authorises the payer to deduct TDS at a reduced rate — or not at all — for a specific payee, usually because their actual tax liability is lower than what standard TDS would deduct. Enter the certificate rate and this calculator applies it in place of the standard rate. / Section 197 के तहत Assessing Officer द्वारा जारी certificate, जो कम या शून्य rate पर TDS काटने की अनुमति देता है।

What is Form 15G/15H and when does it apply?

Form 15G (for those below 60) and Form 15H (for senior citizens) are self-declarations that the declarant's total income is expected to remain below the taxable threshold, submitted to avoid TDS on interest or dividend income. This calculator zeroes TDS for 194A/194 when the checkbox is ticked, but eligibility remains the declarant's own responsibility to confirm. / Form 15G (60 से कम) और 15H (senior citizen) self-declarations हैं जो interest/dividend पर TDS से बचने के लिए जमा किए जाते हैं।

Does the calculator account for GST when computing TDS?

Yes — if you tick "GST Included in Amount," the calculator backs out the GST component (at the rate you specify, default 18%) before computing TDS, since CBDT clarifies that TDS should be calculated on the amount excluding GST when GST is shown separately on the invoice. / GST Included टिक करने पर calculator TDS की गणना से पहले GST component हटा देता है।

What is Multiple Payment Mode?

It lets you add unlimited payment rows with their own dates and amounts, tracking the cumulative total against the section's threshold and applying TDS — including a one-time catch-up on the crossing payment — exactly as it evolves for a payee who receives several payments across the year. / यह असीमित payment rows जोड़ने देता है और threshold पार होने पर TDS की गणना करता है।

What does "Auto-generate from Payment Details" do?

It builds a full set of payment rows automatically using the Gross Amount, Number of Payments and Payment Frequency you've entered in the Payment Details tab, saving you from typing each row by hand for a simple recurring schedule. / यह Payment Details tab के आँकड़ों से payment rows स्वचालित रूप से बना देता है।

Can I calculate TDS for NRI (non-resident) payments?

Selecting "Non-Resident (NRI)" shows a note that cross-border payments are generally governed by Section 195 (rates under the Act or the applicable DTAA), not the domestic rates shown here — this calculator's domestic-rate engine is a reference point only for such payments, not a substitute for Section 195 analysis. / NRI चुनने पर एक नोट दिखता है कि cross-border भुगतान आमतौर पर Section 195 के तहत आते हैं, domestic rates के तहत नहीं।

What is Form 16?

The annual TDS certificate an employer issues to an employee for salary TDS deducted under Section 192 across the whole financial year, used when filing the employee's income tax return. / Employer द्वारा जारी वार्षिक salary TDS certificate।

What is Form 16A?

The TDS certificate for all non-salary deductions — contractor payments, professional fees, rent, interest, commission and the rest — issued quarterly by the deductor. / गैर-salary TDS deductions के लिए तिमाही certificate।

What is Form 26AS / AIS?

Form 26AS is the consolidated tax-credit statement showing every TDS/TCS entry recorded against your PAN; it's now supplemented by the more detailed Annual Information Statement (AIS), and both should be reconciled against your own records before filing your return. / यह आपके PAN पर दर्ज हर TDS/TCS entry दिखाने वाला consolidated statement है।

What is TRACES?

TRACES (TDS Reconciliation Analysis and Correction Enabling System) is the official portal where deductors file TDS returns and generate Form 16/16A, and deductees can view, verify and reconcile their TDS credits online. / यह TDS returns file करने और certificates बनाने के लिए सरकारी portal है।

How do I get a TDS refund if too much was deducted?

File your income tax return, reporting the TDS shown in your Form 26AS/AIS against your actual tax liability for the year — if TDS exceeds what you actually owe, the excess is refunded directly to your bank account after the return is processed. / अपना income tax return file करें; ज़्यादा कटे TDS का refund बैंक खाते में सीधे जमा होता है।

What are the benefits of TDS?

It spreads tax collection evenly through the year for the government, creates a documented, traceable paper trail for both payer and payee, and often reduces or eliminates the payee's own advance-tax or self-assessment burden since a large share of their liability is already collected upfront. / यह tax collection को पूरे साल फैला देता है और एक दस्तावेज़ी trail बनाता है।

What common mistakes does this calculator help avoid?

Deducting TDS on the GST-inclusive amount instead of the taxable base; missing an aggregate threshold crossed by several small payments; forgetting the Section 206AA higher rate when PAN is missing; and applying the standard rate despite a valid Section 197 certificate or Form 15G/15H on file. / GST-सहित राशि पर TDS काटना, aggregate threshold भूल जाना, PAN न होने पर higher rate भूल जाना जैसी ग़लतियों से बचाता है।

Is TDS the same as income tax?

No — TDS is a mechanism for collecting income tax in advance at the point of payment; the amount deducted is credited against the payee's total income tax liability for the year when they file their return, not a separate tax in itself. / TDS अलग tax नहीं बल्कि income tax को पहले से इकट्ठा करने की व्यवस्था है।

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • TDS shows ₹0 even though I entered an amount: the taxable amount hasn't crossed the applicable threshold for that section yet — check the "TDS Engine Determination" panel for the exact threshold being applied.
  • The rate looks higher than the standard rate: check whether "PAN Not Available" is selected — Section 206AA raises the rate to the higher of 20% or the standard rate once the threshold is crossed.
  • Salary Details tab is empty or hidden: it only applies to Section 192 — switch the TDS Type to "Salary" to access it.
  • Section-specific fields aren't showing: some sections (194H, 194, 194IA, 194O) have no extra fields beyond Payment Details — that's expected, not a bug.
  • Multiple Payment table shows no rows: tick "Enable multiple payment mode," then use "Add Payment Row" or "Auto-generate from Payment Details."
  • PDF or Excel export does nothing: these features load a small external library the first time you use them — check your internet connection and try again.
  • Light mode looks odd on the site header or sidebar: by design, the Light/Dark toggle only affects the calculator's own panels, keeping the site's shared dark navigation consistent across every tool.
  • Charts appear empty: a chart with no data (for example, zero gross amount) correctly shows "No data to display" rather than a misleading empty circle.
