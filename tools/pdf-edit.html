ℹ️ About This Tool

About the Edit PDF Tool (English)

PDF is a fixed-layout format, which is exactly why it is so useful for sharing — and exactly why it can be frustrating to change. Most people do not need to rewrite the underlying document; they need to add something to it: fill in a blank on a form, sign or initial a page, highlight an important clause, draw an arrow to point something out, drop in a logo, or stamp a document as approved. The ApneSoftware Edit PDF tool is built for exactly this. It is a browser-based editor that lets you place new content on top of any PDF and arrange it precisely, then download the result — all without installing software or uploading your file.

The tool offers a full palette of objects. You can add text boxes and type in them directly, choosing the font, size, weight (bold), style (italic), alignment and colour. Importantly, the text is rendered exactly as you see it and supports any language, including Hindi and other Indian scripts, Arabic, Chinese, Japanese and emoji — solving a common limitation of simpler PDF tools that can only add basic Latin characters. For marking up documents there are shapes — rectangle, rounded rectangle and ellipse, each with an outline or a solid fill — plus lines and arrows for pointing things out, a highlighter for emphasising passages, and a freehand pen for drawing or scribbling a quick note.

Beyond drawing, you can insert images — a PNG or JPG logo, a photo, or a picture of your signature — and place, scale and rotate them anywhere. Ready-made stamps (Approved, Draft, Confidential and Paid) let you flag a document's status in one click, styled as bold coloured labels. Every object you add becomes a movable item: drag it to reposition, use the corner handle to resize, use the round handle to rotate it to any angle, and adjust its colour, opacity and stroke width in the properties panel. You can duplicate an object, lock it so it will not move by accident, and change its stacking order by bringing it forward or sending it backward relative to other objects.

Editing a real document means working across pages and making mistakes, so the tool is built around that. A thumbnail sidebar shows every page and lets you jump between them; each object remembers which page it belongs to, so your edits stay put as you navigate. Previous and next controls, a page indicator and zoom help you work comfortably on large or dense pages. And because nobody gets it right the first time, full undo and redo let you step back and forward through your changes without fear.

A word on how the editing works technically, because it shapes what you can expect. This tool adds a new layer of content on top of the existing page; it does not rewrite or reflow the text that is already in the PDF. That is the right model for the vast majority of real tasks — annotating, filling, signing and marking up — and it means your original page is never altered or degraded underneath your additions. When you save, your added objects are rendered at high resolution and drawn onto each page exactly as they appear on screen, so the output is a faithful, what-you-see-is-what-you-get copy of your edits.

When you are finished, the output options let you rename the file and optionally compress it, then download the edited PDF or send it straight to your printer. Everything is baked into a standard PDF that opens and prints correctly in any reader. Password-protected PDFs you are authorised to open are handled locally, and large multi-page documents are supported, processed on your own machine.

Privacy is fundamental. Your PDF, any images you add and every edit you make are processed entirely on your own device using JavaScript; nothing is uploaded, stored or logged, and everything is cleared from memory when you close the page. That makes the editor safe for contracts, application forms, financial statements, medical documents and any other confidential file — the kind of documents you would never want to send to an unknown server just to add a line of text. There is no account to create, no software to install and no watermark added to your work.

Whether you are filling in a government form in Hindi, signing and dating an agreement, highlighting the key terms of a contract, adding your company logo to a proposal, marking a draft for review, or simply correcting a small detail visually, the Edit PDF tool gives you a fast, private and capable way to change a PDF without specialist software.

📝 How To Use

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🔒 Privacy

This tool is 100% client-side. Your PDF, any images you add and all your edits are processed entirely in your browser using JavaScript — they are never sent to ApneSoftware or any third party. Nothing is stored, logged or transmitted. When you close or refresh the page, everything is cleared from memory. Because there is no upload, you can safely edit confidential, legal, medical and financial documents.

Works in all modern browsers including Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, Brave and Opera, on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. It relies on standard web technologies (Canvas, File API and JavaScript PDF libraries), so no plugins or extensions are required. Touch screens are supported for adding and moving objects.

Input document: PDF (.pdf), including password-protected PDFs you are authorised to open. Added images: PNG and JPG/JPEG. Output: a standard PDF (.pdf) with your text, shapes, images and stamps drawn onto the chosen pages, ready to download or print. Added content sits on a new layer over the original page.
