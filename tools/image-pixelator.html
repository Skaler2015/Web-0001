ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Image Pixelator is a professional, privacy-first tool for hiding faces and sensitive details in a photo — directly in your browser. Every day we share images that contain more than we intend: a bystander's face in a holiday snap, a car number plate in a photo of a new vehicle, a delivery label with a home address, a signature on a signed document, an account number on a screenshot, or a friend who did not consent to being posted. This tool lets you permanently obscure any of those details in seconds by covering them with a chunky pixel mosaic, a soft blur, or a solid black redaction bar — the same techniques television and newspapers have used for decades — and it does it all locally, with nothing uploaded to a server.

The workspace is built around fast, accurate selection tools. Choose the rectangle for plates, labels and text blocks; the ellipse for faces and round objects; or the brush to paint freely over irregular shapes such as hair, tattoos or handwriting. You simply drag over the area you want to hide and the effect is applied instantly. Because you can draw as many times as you like, a single photo can have every face and every private detail covered without ever leaving the page. A live selection outline shows exactly what will be affected before you commit, and precise zoom controls let you place edits pixel-by-pixel on small details and then fit the whole image back on screen.

Three effect styles cover every situation. The mosaic style averages each block of the region into a single colour, producing the classic censored, blocky look; a block-size slider controls how coarse it is, and larger blocks make the underlying detail more thoroughly unreadable. The blur style applies a smooth Gaussian-style softening with an adjustable radius — useful when you want a gentler, less attention-grabbing cover. The black bar style paints a solid opaque rectangle for hard redaction of text and numbers, the safest option when a detail must be completely gone. Two extra controls refine the result: feather softens the edge of each edit so it blends into the surrounding image instead of showing a hard rectangle, and opacity lets you apply a partial cover when you want the shape to remain suggested rather than fully hidden.

Crucially, this pixelation is genuinely irreversible. Some online editors simply place a blurred layer on top of the original, which can sometimes be peeled back or reversed; here, the pixelated or blurred pixels actually replace the original pixels on the canvas, so once you export the image the hidden information is destroyed and cannot be recovered from the file. That distinction matters enormously for privacy: a face you mosaic here is gone for good in the downloaded copy. For extra safety the tool never writes the original pixels into the export, and because everything runs in your browser, the sensitive image is never transmitted anywhere in the first place.

A full history system supports comfortable editing. Every edit can be reversed with undo and reapplied with redo, a reset restores the untouched original at any time, and a pixelate whole image action censors the entire photo in one click — handy for creating a blurred thumbnail or a privacy placeholder. A before/after compare button reveals the untouched original whenever you hold it, so you can confirm you have covered everything you meant to. When you are done, export to PNG (lossless), JPG (small, ideal for sharing) or WebP (the best size-to-quality balance), each rendered at the image's full native resolution with a quality slider, and an image-information panel reports dimensions, megapixels, aspect ratio and source file size so you always know what you are producing.

Privacy is the entire point of this tool, so it is designed around it. Unlike many online blur tools that upload your photo to a server to process it, this one does everything locally. Your image is read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, edited and handed straight back to you — it never leaves your device, there is no account, and nothing about your image is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for the most sensitive material: medical documents, legal paperwork, ID cards, children's faces and confidential screenshots. It keeps working offline once the page has loaded, and the interface follows modern accessibility standards with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines and high-contrast support. Note that this is a genuine, manual, deterministic pixelation tool — there are no AI features and no automatic face detection. You choose exactly what to hide, which means nothing is ever missed by a faulty detector and nothing is ever sent to a model. In short, it is a fast, capable and genuinely private censoring studio that lives entirely in your browser.

🛡️ Privacy Editing Guide

  • Faces: use the ellipse with a large mosaic block, and cover a little extra around the hairline and chin.
  • License plates & text: use the rectangle with a black bar or a coarse mosaic so characters are unreadable.
  • Signatures & handwriting: use the brush to follow the strokes, or a rectangle over the whole signature.
  • ID / account numbers: the black bar is safest — a light blur can sometimes still be guessed.
  • QR & barcodes: cover the entire code with a black bar; partial blur can leave it scannable.
  • Check your work: zoom in and use before/after compare to be sure nothing sensitive peeks out.

💾 Export Guide

  • PNG — lossless; the safest choice for redacted text and sharp edges.
  • JPG — smaller files, perfect for sharing photos on chat and social apps.
  • WebP — smaller than JPG at similar quality in modern browsers.
  • Quality slider — affects JPG and WebP; 90–95% is a good default.
  • Exports are rendered at full resolution and the hidden pixels are permanently replaced.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Detail is still readable: increase the block size (mosaic) or use a black bar for total redaction.
  • Edit looks like a hard rectangle: increase Feather to blend the edge into the photo.
  • Brush covers too much or too little: adjust the Brush size slider before painting.
  • Covered the wrong spot: use Undo, or Reset to start over from the original.
  • Downloaded JPG looks slightly soft: raise the quality slider or export as PNG.
  • WebP export unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG or JPG.
  • Can the blur be reversed? No — the pixels are replaced in the export, so the detail is gone for good.
