ℹ️ About This Tool — Free Online Text to Speech

This Text to Speech tool turns any written text into natural spoken audio that plays instantly in your web browser. It is a completely free, online Text to Speech tool that uses your browser's built-in Speech Synthesis engine, so there is no software to install, no account to create and nothing to pay. Whether you want to listen to an article while you rest your eyes, proofread your own writing by ear, or study on the go, this browser text reader lets you read text aloud in seconds.

What This Tool Does

At its core, the tool takes the text you type, paste or import and speaks it out loud using the voices available on your device. You can choose from every voice your browser and operating system provide, change the reading speed, adjust the pitch and control the volume. As it reads, the current sentence and word are highlighted so you can follow along easily, which is perfect for reading practice, language learning and accessibility.

Why It Is Useful

Listening is often faster and less tiring than reading, especially for long documents. A good text reader lets you consume content while doing other things, catch mistakes you would skim past on screen, and give your eyes a break. People who find reading difficult — because of dyslexia, low vision or simply tiredness — can absorb the same information comfortably through audio. Turning text into speech also makes multitasking easy: you can listen to your notes while commuting, cooking or exercising.

Key Features

  • Natural voices from your browser and device, with search, language, accent and gender filters.
  • Adjustable speed, pitch and volume, plus a voice preview and favourite voices.
  • Full playback control: play, pause, resume, stop, restart, repeat and infinite loop.
  • Live sentence and word highlighting with auto-scroll in the reading view.
  • Import text from TXT, PDF and DOCX files, or drag and drop a text file.
  • Character, word, sentence and paragraph counters with reading and speaking time.
  • Sleep timer, download as .txt, print, auto-save and a recent history list.

Benefits of Using Read Text Aloud

Hearing your own writing read back to you is one of the best ways to catch awkward sentences, missing words and typos. Students can revise by listening to notes, writers can polish drafts, and busy professionals can get through reports hands-free. Because the tool works entirely in the browser, it is fast, responsive and available whenever you need it.

How to Use

  1. Type or paste your text into the editor, or import a TXT, PDF or DOCX file.
  2. Pick a voice and adjust the speed, pitch and volume to suit you.
  3. Press Play to hear the text read aloud with live highlighting.
  4. Use pause, resume, stop, repeat or loop as needed, and set a sleep timer if you like.
  5. Download or print the text, and revisit earlier text from your recent history.

Why Choose This Tool?

Many online readers send your text to a remote server, require a login or limit how much you can convert. This free Text to Speech tool does the opposite: it is unlimited, needs no signup, contains no ads-driven paywall and processes everything on your own device. It combines a clean, modern interface with genuinely useful features such as word highlighting, file import and playback control that many simple readers lack.

Privacy & Security

Your privacy is fully protected because all processing happens locally in your browser using the native Speech Synthesis API. The text you enter is never uploaded to any server, never stored in the cloud and never shared with third parties. Imported files are read directly in your browser and are not transmitted anywhere. When you close the tab, the audio stops and nothing is left behind except the optional auto-saved copy kept only on your own device.

Supported Browsers

The tool works in every modern browser that supports the Web Speech API, including Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Apple Safari, Mozilla Firefox and Opera, on both desktop and mobile. The exact voices available depend on your operating system, so Windows, macOS, Android and iOS each offer their own set of voices. Chrome and Edge usually provide the widest choice, including online neural voices when you are connected.

Tips for Best Results

  • Break very long text into paragraphs so highlighting and scrolling stay smooth.
  • Try a few voices — quality varies a lot between devices and languages.
  • Slightly slower speeds (0.8x–0.9x) sound clearer for study and language practice.
  • Use punctuation properly; commas and full stops help the voice pause naturally.

Limitations of Browser Voices

Because this tool uses the voices built into your browser and device, the number and quality of voices are not the same for everyone. Some devices offer only one or two robotic voices, while others include many lifelike ones. A few browsers also pause long readings, which is why the tool automatically splits your text into smaller pieces for reliable playback.

Conclusion

This online Text to Speech tool gives you a fast, private and flexible way to listen to any text without installing anything or trusting a server with your words. With natural voices, precise controls, file import and live highlighting, it is a dependable everyday companion for reading, studying, proofreading and accessibility — completely free.
