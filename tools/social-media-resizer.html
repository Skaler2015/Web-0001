ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Social Media Image Resizer is a professional, privacy-first studio for resizing images to the exact pixel dimensions every social platform expects — directly in your browser. Every network crops, compresses or letterboxes an image that is the wrong size, and the result is a blurry profile picture, a cover photo with someone's head cut off, or a thumbnail that looks amateur. The specifications change often and differ for each platform, which makes "what size should this be?" one of the most common and frustrating questions for anyone posting online. This tool answers it in one click: choose the platform and placement, and your image is fitted to precisely the right dimensions — and it does everything locally, so your photos never leave your device.

At its core is a large, up-to-date library of platform presets. In one menu you will find the sizes for Instagram (profile, square, portrait and landscape posts, stories, reels and highlight covers), Facebook (profile, cover, story, post, event and group covers), YouTube (thumbnail, channel banner, Shorts cover and community post), LinkedIn (banner, profile and post), X / Twitter (header, profile and post), Pinterest (pin and board cover), TikTok, WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, Threads, Google Business, Amazon and Shopify. Pick one and the canvas instantly becomes that exact size. When you need something unusual, a custom size lets you type any width and height, lock the aspect ratio so one dimension follows the other, and even work in inches, centimetres or millimetres with a DPI setting for print-style sizing.

Fitting a photo into a new shape is where most resizers fall short, so this one gives you three modes and real control. Fill scales the image to cover the whole frame and crops the overflow — and crucially, you can drag the image to choose which part stays in view, so a face or product is never chopped off. Fit scales the whole image to sit inside the frame without cropping, filling the leftover space with a background of your choice. Stretch forces the image to the exact box when you truly need to. For the padding behind a fitted image you can choose white, black, any solid colour, a two-colour gradient, full transparency (for a PNG), or a beautifully blurred version of your own photo — the popular look that fills a square feed post with a soft, on-brand backdrop instead of hard bars.

Beyond sizing, the studio includes the finishing touches you would otherwise open another app for. Quick image adjustments — brightness, contrast and saturation — and one-tap filters such as grayscale, sepia, vintage, matte, cinema and HDR let you set a consistent mood across a set of posts. A watermark adds your brand or handle as text, with control over position, colour and opacity, so every image you publish is protected and recognisable. A before/after compare button and a full-screen view let you check the result closely, and everything updates live on the canvas as you work.

Batch processing turns a chore into a moment. Add several images at once, choose a preset and your settings, and the tool resizes them all and packages the results into a single ZIP download — ideal for preparing a carousel, a product catalogue or a week of posts in one go. Each image is fitted with the same mode and background so the set looks cohesive. And when you export, you control the output: choose JPG (small, ideal for photos), PNG (lossless, and transparent when you want it) or WebP (the best size-to-quality balance), each with a quality slider, all rendered at the preset's full resolution. You can also copy a single result to the clipboard to paste straight into a post.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online resizers that upload your image to a server to process it — often the very images you are about to make public, but sometimes private drafts too — this tool does everything locally. Your image is read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, resized and handed straight back to you; it never leaves your device, there is no account, and nothing about your image is tracked or stored. Your preferred settings are remembered only in your own browser. That makes it safe for personal photos, client work and brand assets alike, and it keeps working offline once loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines and high-contrast support. Note that this is a genuine, deterministic resizing tool — there are no AI features; every crop, pad and filter is a precise, repeatable operation you control, so the output is exactly what you set. Because every preset, mode and background updates the canvas instantly, you can compare options side by side and settle on the best framing in seconds rather than minutes. In short, it is a fast, capable and genuinely private resize studio that lives entirely in your browser — powerful enough for a full content batch, simple enough for a single profile picture.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Important part is cropped: in Fill mode, drag the image to reposition the crop.
  • Image has bars around it: that's Fit mode — switch to Fill, or set a colour/blur background.
  • Result looks stretched: you're in Stretch mode — use Fill or Fit to keep proportions.
  • Need a transparent background: use Fit with a Transparent background and export PNG.
  • Custom cm/inch size looks wrong: check the DPI — 96 for screen, 300 for print.
  • WebP export unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG or JPG.
  • Batch ZIP didn't download: allow downloads for the site and make sure you added more than one image.
