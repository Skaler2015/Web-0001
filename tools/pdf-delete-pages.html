ℹ️ About This Tool

About the PDF Delete Pages Tool (English)

The PDF Delete Pages tool by ApneSoftware lets you remove unwanted pages from any PDF — a single stray page, a scattered set, or a whole block — directly inside your web browser. You see a thumbnail of every page, mark the ones you want gone, and download a clean, correctly ordered PDF, all without uploading your file to any server.

Introduction

Not every page in a PDF deserves to stay. A blank sheet left over from double-sided scanning, an outdated cover letter, a duplicate page that was scanned twice, or a confidential internal section you do not want to share — these are everyday reasons to trim a document. Working from page numbers alone is error-prone, especially in long files. This tool shows you exactly what each page looks like, so you can select the right ones with confidence and review your choice before anything is removed.

Main Features

  • Drag & drop upload with a loading bar and a file-info panel (name, size, page count, title and a live count of pages marked for deletion).
  • Thumbnail preview of every page, plus a full-screen preview with zoom, fit-width, fit-page and page navigation.
  • Smart selection: click to mark, Ctrl/Cmd-click to toggle one, Shift-click to mark a range.
  • Quick selectors: select all, invert, odd, even, first, last or a custom range like 2-4,7.
  • Automatic detection of blank pages and duplicate (repeated) pages for fast clean-up.
  • Undo, redo and reset so you can experiment safely before saving.
  • Output control: custom file name, preserve or remove metadata, and optional compression.
  • Download or print the trimmed PDF directly.

Benefits

The tool is fast, private and precise. Because you can see every page, you never delete the wrong one; because remaining pages are simply copied, their content, formatting and order are perfectly preserved with no quality loss; and because everything runs locally, there is no upload wait and no privacy risk. It is completely free, adds no watermark, needs no account, and places no limit on the number of pages or files.

Why Use This Tool

Many online page removers upload your PDF to a remote server, where a copy could be logged. This one processes everything on your device, so confidential contracts, financial statements and identity documents never leave it. It also offers more than a basic remover — multi-select with Ctrl/Shift, odd/even/range selectors, blank and duplicate detection, undo/redo and a proper full-screen preview — in a clean, modern interface that works on any device.

Common Use Cases

  • Removing blank scanner pages inserted between double-sided scans.
  • Deleting an outdated cover letter or terms page before sending a contract.
  • Cutting a confidential internal section out of a report before sharing.
  • Trimming duplicate pages that were scanned or merged twice.
  • Cleaning up a PDF that accumulated extra pages over several edits.

Privacy & Security

Privacy is built in. Your PDF is read with the browser's File API, previewed with a client-side PDF engine and rewritten with a client-side PDF library; nothing is transmitted, cached on a server or kept after you close the tab, and temporary data is released from memory when you reset. This makes the tool suitable for personal, business, legal, financial and medical documents that should never pass through a third party.

Supported Formats & Browser Compatibility

The tool works with standard PDF files and outputs a standard PDF. It runs in the latest Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Opera and Brave on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. A modern browser with JavaScript is all you need — nothing to install.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Open a page full-screen if you are unsure whether it should be removed.
  • Use “Mark blank” to catch empty scanner pages you might otherwise miss.
  • Use the range box (e.g. 5-9) to target a block of pages quickly.
  • Keep “Preserve metadata” on unless you specifically want to strip it.
  • Review the “Marked to delete” count before saving so nothing extra is removed.

Performance, Accessibility & Limitations

Pages are rendered one at a time as compact thumbnails, and full-resolution rendering only happens in the preview, keeping memory use low even for long documents. There is no upload round-trip, so the tool feels instant, and it produces the trimmed PDF locally the moment you save. Every control is keyboard-reachable with clear labels, visible focus outlines and strong contrast in both themes, and there are shortcuts for undo, redo, save and delete. As for limits, blank and duplicate detection are heuristic aids (based on the rendered image) rather than a guarantee — always glance at the marked pages — and at least one page must remain in the file.

Who Is It For

The tool suits everyone who handles PDFs day to day: students trimming a syllabus or removing blank pages from scanned notes; office staff cleaning up reports and contracts before circulation; accountants dropping duplicate statement pages; legal teams removing a confidential exhibit before disclosure; teachers and administrators tidying scanned forms; and anyone who has ever received a PDF padded with cover sheets, blank separators or accidental repeats. Because it needs no installation and no account, it is equally handy for a one-off fix on a public computer and for repeated clean-up as part of a daily document workflow.

Conclusion

PDF Delete Pages combines a clear visual workflow with a strict privacy model — free and entirely in the browser. From one stray page to a large block, it gives you fast, safe, lossless control over your document without your file ever leaving your device. It turns a fiddly clean-up chore into a few confident clicks, and scales just as happily from a two-page form to a several-hundred-page archive.
