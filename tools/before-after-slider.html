ℹ️ About This Tool

The Professional Image Comparison Suite is a free, browser-based application for comparing two images with precision and clarity. It began life as a simple before/after slider and has grown into a full comparison workspace that photographers, designers, developers, editors, marketers, students, agencies, architects and e-commerce sellers can rely on for day-to-day visual review work. The guiding idea is simple: comparing images should be fast, honest and completely private, and you should never have to hand your photos to a third-party server just to see how two versions differ.

At the heart of the tool is a hardware-accelerated HTML5 canvas engine. When you load a Before image and an After image, both are decoded locally and drawn onto a single canvas, aligned to a shared frame so that every comparison is fair. From there you choose how to compare. The horizontal and vertical sliders give you the familiar wipe reveal — drag the handle and watch one image dissolve into the other along a straight edge. The circle reveal mode paints the After image inside a circle whose radius you control, which is perfect for checking a single spot such as a removed blemish or a replaced logo. Split view locks the comparison at the centre and shows the left half of one image beside the right half of the other, while side by side shrinks both full images into the same frame for a quick overview. For photographic work, the overlay mode stacks the images with an adjustable opacity slider and a set of blend modes — multiply, screen, overlay, difference, darken and lighten — that reveal tonal and colour shifts that a wipe would hide. Finally, fade cross-dissolves between the two images so you can present an edit smoothly, almost like a slow animation you drive by hand.

Comparison is only half the story; understanding how much changed is the other half. That is why the suite includes an objective difference analysis engine. With a single click it samples both images at a matched resolution, computes the absolute per-pixel difference across the red, green and blue channels, and reports three headline numbers: the overall difference percentage, the count of pixels that changed beyond a sensible threshold (the "changed area"), and the mean pixel delta. It also paints a change heat-map where brighter regions mark bigger changes, and draws an RGB histogram that overlays the tonal distribution of both images so you can see, for instance, that an edit lifted the shadows or cooled the whites. For a QA engineer chasing a visual regression, or a photographer justifying a retouch, these numbers turn a vague impression into evidence.

Getting images into the tool is deliberately flexible. You can click to browse, drag and drop files straight from your desktop, or paste an image directly from your clipboard after a screenshot. On a phone or tablet you can capture a photo with the camera on the spot. You can also import an image by URL, which is handy when you want to compare something already published online. Each side accepts multiple images, so you can load a set of Before candidates and a set of After candidates and flip between them using the thumbnail strips without re-uploading anything. Because the whole engine is client-side, switching images is instantaneous.

Once you have a comparison you like, precise inspection tools take over. Zoom presets of 50%, 100%, 200% and 400% sit next to fine-grained mouse-wheel and pinch zoom, and when you are zoomed in the hand tool lets you pan around the image while the slider still works. The double-click and Reset controls snap everything back to a centred, 100% view. You can rename the labels that sit in the corners — Before/After, Original/Edited, Version 1/Version 2, or any custom text — and those labels are baked into whatever you export. Speaking of export, the composed view can be saved as a PNG (with transparency), a JPG or WebP (flattened onto your chosen background), a single-page PDF ready for printing or emailing, or an SVG wrapper. A full-resolution option renders the export at the native size of your source images rather than at screen size, so printed comparisons stay crisp.

Throughout, the tool respects your privacy and your time. There is no account, no watermark, no upload and no tracking of your image content. It works offline once loaded, it is fully keyboard accessible, and it is built to be light enough to run comfortably on a mid-range phone. In short, it aims to be the last image-comparison tool you need to bookmark — powerful enough for professional review, simple enough to hand to a client, and trustworthy enough to use with sensitive material.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • An AVIF/TIFF/BMP image won't load: your browser may not decode that format. Convert it to PNG or JPG first with the Image Converter.
  • Export or Difference is blocked after a URL import: the remote image is cross-origin without CORS. Download it and upload the file instead.
  • The two images look misaligned: they have different aspect ratios. Enable Match aspect for a strict overlay, or crop them to the same ratio with the Image Cropper.
  • The slider feels stuck at high zoom: you may be in the hand-pan area — press Reset or double-click to recentre.
  • A very large image is slow: resize it first with the Image Resizer; the difference engine already samples down automatically for speed.
  • Nothing happens on Paste: make sure an image (not a file path) is actually on your clipboard, and that the page has focus.
