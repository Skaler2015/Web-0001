ℹ️ About This Tool

Image to PDF Converter (English)

The Image to PDF Converter by ApneSoftware turns one or many pictures into a single, tidy PDF document — right inside your web browser. Whether you are scanning receipts with your phone, saving a set of photographed notebook pages, assembling a photo portfolio, or bundling identity documents for an application, this tool combines your images into a professional PDF with precise control over layout, quality and security, and without ever uploading a file to a server.

Introduction

Images arrive in many shapes and formats, and sharing a folder of loose JPGs or screenshots is rarely convenient. A PDF solves that: it keeps pages in a fixed order, opens the same way on every device, and is easy to email, print or upload to a portal. This converter takes the hassle out of that final step. You add your images, arrange them, choose how they should sit on the page, and download a finished PDF in seconds — a small task you might repeat weekly that this tool makes effortless.

Features

  • Flexible upload: drag & drop or click to add multiple images at once.
  • Wide format support: JPG, PNG, WEBP, BMP, GIF, TIFF and HEIC where your browser can decode them.
  • Image management: reorder by dragging, move first or last, duplicate, reverse and delete.
  • Page settings: A3, A4, A5, Letter, Legal, Executive, Tabloid, B5, match-image or a fully custom size.
  • Orientation & margins: portrait, landscape or auto, with none, small, medium, large or custom margins.
  • Placement modes: fit, fill, stretch or original size.
  • Quality & compression: 10–100% quality and five compression levels.
  • Page extras: page numbers, header, footer and a coloured border.
  • PDF details: custom file name plus title, author, subject and keywords.
  • Security: open and owner passwords, with optional print and copy restrictions.

Benefits

The biggest benefit is convenience combined with privacy. Instead of installing heavy desktop software or trusting an unknown website with sensitive scans, you get a fast, capable converter that keeps everything on your own machine. You save time, avoid sending large batches of image attachments, and produce a single file that looks professional and is easy for the recipient to handle.

Real-life Use Cases

  • Turning phone photos of receipts and bills into one PDF for an expense claim.
  • Combining scanned ID pages, certificates or forms for an online application.
  • Saving a set of whiteboard or notebook photos as tidy study notes.
  • Assembling product shots or design mock-ups into a shareable catalogue.
  • Packaging screenshots into a single report or bug log.
  • Creating a printable photo album or handout from a folder of pictures.

Advantages, Supported Formats & Performance

Because the conversion runs on your device, there is no upload or download round-trip, which makes it fast even on a modest connection and usable offline once loaded. Images are rasterised efficiently and embedded at the quality you choose, so you decide the balance between crisp detail and small file size. The tool accepts the formats most people use every day and gracefully tells you when a rare format cannot be decoded by your browser, so you are never left guessing.

Privacy & Security

Privacy is central to the design. Your images are read with the browser's File API and converted with a client-side PDF engine; nothing is transmitted, cached on a server or retained after you close the tab. For confidential material, you can add a password so the PDF can only be opened with it, and you can restrict printing or copying so the content is harder to reuse. This makes the tool suitable for personal documents, business paperwork and anything you would not want passing through a third-party server.

Browser Compatibility

The converter works in the latest versions of Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Opera and Brave, on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. A modern browser with JavaScript enabled is all that is required — there is nothing to install and no plugin to configure.

Why Choose This Tool

Many online converters upload your files, limit the number of images, stamp a watermark, or hide useful options behind a paywall. This tool does none of that. It is free and unlimited, it never uploads your images, and it offers professional-grade controls — page size, placement, quality, borders, metadata and password protection — in a clean, fast, modern interface that works on any device.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use the highest-resolution images you have for the sharpest print results.
  • Choose Fit to keep photos undistorted, and Fill only when you want images to cover the whole page.
  • Turn compression up when emailing, and off when you need maximum quality.
  • For scanned documents, a small margin and a white background usually look best.
  • Set the file name and metadata so the PDF is easy to find and identify later.

Common Mistakes

  • Uploading tiny thumbnails and expecting print-quality output — start from large images.
  • Using Stretch on photos, which distorts them; prefer Fit or Fill.
  • Setting maximum compression for documents that need to stay legible.
  • Forgetting the page order — arrange the images before creating the PDF.
