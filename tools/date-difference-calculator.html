⚙️ How it works

Every calculation uses your browser's native date engine, which already understands month lengths, leap years and time zones. For the calendar difference the tool counts whole years, then whole months, then the remaining days, borrowing from the previous month when needed — the same way people describe durations in plain language ("2 years, 3 months, 10 days"). Alongside that human-readable breakdown it computes the raw totals (total days, weeks, hours and so on) by taking the exact millisecond gap between the two moments and dividing it down. Business-day counting walks day by day from the start to the end, skipping any weekday you mark as a weekend and any date in your holiday list.

🏢 Business days

A calendar difference includes weekends and holidays, but payroll, SLAs, courts and offices usually run on working days. This tool counts only the days that matter: choose which weekdays are your weekend (Saturday and/or Sunday, or a six-day school week), paste your public and company holidays, and it returns the exact number of business days between two dates. Presets for office (Mon–Fri), school (Mon–Sat) and government (Mon–Fri) set sensible defaults you can still adjust.

🔁 Leap year handling

A year is a leap year if it is divisible by 4, except century years, which must be divisible by 400 — so 2000 was a leap year but 1900 was not. Because the calculator uses the browser's date engine, February 29th, leap days and month-length differences are handled automatically, and the Difference tab tells you exactly how many leap years fall inside your range and how many extra days they contribute.

ℹ️ About This Tool

The Professional Date & Time Calculator is a free, browser-based tool that answers, precisely and instantly, almost any question that involves dates and time. It began as a simple "difference between two dates" calculator and grew into a five-mode workbench because date arithmetic, in real life, is rarely just one calculation. People need to know an exact age for a form, the number of working days until a deadline, the date thirty business days from now, how long a project has run, or how many days remain until an exam or a wedding — and each of those is a slightly different problem. This tool gathers them into one clean interface so you never have to hunt for the right calculator or risk an off-by-one mistake counting on your fingers.

The default Difference mode does what the original tool did, only more thoroughly. Pick a start date and an end date and it returns the gap in the natural years-months-days form that matches how durations are written in official documents, and — because that human breakdown is not always what you need — it also gives the raw totals: total days, weeks, hours, minutes, seconds and even milliseconds. You can switch on time-of-day to include hours and minutes for precise, sub-day durations. It works no matter which date you enter first, tells you if it had to reverse them, and flags edge cases such as the two dates being identical or the range being extremely large. Beneath the numbers it draws a visual timeline from start to end and a mini calendar that highlights the range, weekends and any holidays, so the span is easy to grasp at a glance.

The Add / Subtract mode solves the inverse problem: instead of measuring the gap between two known dates, it takes one date and shifts it. Enter a base date, choose add or subtract, and specify any combination of years, months, weeks, days, hours and minutes to land on the resulting date and time. This is exactly what you need for deadlines ("what date is 45 days from today?"), reminders, contract end dates, or working out when a subscription or notice period expires. Because it uses the browser's date engine, month-length and leap-year adjustments happen automatically, so adding a month to January 31st behaves sensibly rather than producing an impossible date.

For anyone whose deadlines run on the working week, the Business Days mode is the heart of the tool. A plain calendar difference counts every day, but payroll cut-offs, service-level agreements, court and government timelines and office leave are all measured in working days. Here you choose which weekdays count as your weekend — Saturday and Sunday for most offices, or a six-day week for many schools — and paste in your public and company holidays, one date per line. The calculator then walks the range day by day and returns the exact number of business days, excluding both weekends and your holidays, with office, school and government presets to start from. This turns a tedious, error-prone manual count into a single reliable number.

The Age mode is built for the most common date question of all. Enter a date of birth and it shows the exact current age in years, months and days, the day of the week you were born, your zodiac sign, and a live countdown to your next birthday. It is equally useful for calculating a child's age for school admission or vaccination schedules, confirming eligibility for exams, jobs or pensions, or simply satisfying curiosity. The Countdown mode extends this to any future moment: pick an event — a birthday, wedding, anniversary, festival, vacation or exam, or a custom label — and the tool counts down to it in real time, updating every second, so you always know exactly how long is left.

Around these modes sit the details that make a tool feel professional. You can choose your date format — DD/MM/YYYY, MM/DD/YYYY or YYYY-MM-DD — and switch the interface and results between English and Hindi. The Difference tab reports the leap years inside your range and the extra days they add, useful for precise interest or duration calculations. Any result can be copied to your clipboard or exported as CSV, Excel or PDF, or printed, which is handy for HR records, legal filings and project documentation. A local history saves your recent calculations and lets you star favourites, all stored only in your own browser. Inputs are validated so empty, invalid or nonsensical dates are caught before they produce a wrong answer. And, as with every tool here, nothing is ever uploaded: your dates — which can be personal or confidential — are processed entirely on your device, the tool keeps working offline once loaded, and closing the tab clears everything. It is lightweight, fast, mobile-friendly and accessible, built to serve students, employees, HR professionals, project managers, accountants, legal users and businesses with equal reliability.

  • Countdown isn't updating: make sure the event date is in the future; past events show "already passed".
