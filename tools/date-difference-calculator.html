📅 Date Difference Calculator

Find out exactly how much time is between two dates.

ℹ️ About This Tool

Counting the days, months, or years between two dates feels like something you should be able to do in your head, but the moment you try it across different month lengths and the occasional leap year, manual counting becomes unreliable fast. This tool takes any two dates and instantly works out the precise difference between them, broken down into years, months, and days, alongside the total number of days and weeks — covering both the human-readable breakdown most people want and the raw day count that's often needed for official or technical purposes.

This kind of precise date math comes up far more often than people expect. Calculating someone's exact age, figuring out how many days remain until a deadline, working out the duration of an employment period or a lease, determining how many days a project has been running, or simply finding out how long it's been since a significant event are all situations where getting the count exactly right matters, and where doing it manually risks an off-by-one error around month boundaries that's surprisingly easy to make.

The years-months-days breakdown is particularly useful for things like calculating a person's tenure at a job, the exact age of a child for medical or school purposes, or the duration of a loan or lease in a format that matches how such things are usually described in official documents. The total day count, on the other hand, is what you typically need for things like calculating per-day rates, working out interest accrual periods, or filling in forms that specifically ask for a number of days rather than a years-and-months description.

Using the tool is as simple as picking a start date and an end date — it works correctly regardless of which date you enter first, automatically handling the calculation either way. The result updates instantly as you change either date, with no calculation delay or page reload. As with every tool on this site, everything happens locally in your browser; the dates you enter are never transmitted anywhere, so you can use it freely for anything from casual curiosity to sensitive calculations involving personal or official dates. It's a small, focused tool, but it removes one of those quietly annoying little arithmetic tasks that comes up far more often than most people plan for, whether at work, while traveling, or just settling a friendly debate about how long ago something actually happened.

How to use:

  1. Pick a start date and an end date.
  2. See the breakdown instantly.
