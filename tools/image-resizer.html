ℹ️ About This Tool — Image Resizer

The Image Resizer is a free, professional online tool that changes the width and height of your images precisely and privately. It runs entirely inside your web browser, so your photos are never uploaded to a server, and it works on any device.

Introduction

From uploading a profile picture to preparing print-ready artwork, resizing images is one of the most common tasks in digital life. The right size makes an image load faster, fit a layout perfectly and meet the requirements of a form or platform. This tool makes resizing simple, exact and completely private.

What is an Image Resizer?

An image resizer changes the pixel dimensions of a picture — making it larger or smaller. Unlike simply displaying an image at a different size, a true resizer re-samples the pixels and saves a new file at the exact dimensions you choose, so the result is the correct size everywhere it is used.

Why Use It

Platforms and printers expect specific sizes. Social networks crop or reject images that are the wrong shape, websites load faster with correctly sized images, and printers need the right dimensions and resolution. Resizing to the correct size before you upload or print saves time, avoids errors and produces a better result.

Features

  • Resize by pixels, percentage, inches, centimetres or millimetres.
  • Lock or unlock the aspect ratio with smart width/height calculation.
  • Ready-made presets for social media, devices and print.
  • Choose the output format (PNG, JPG, WebP, BMP, AVIF) and quality.
  • Rotate, flip and set a background colour for transparent images.
  • Batch resize many images and download them as a ZIP.
  • Before/after comparison, zoom and a full information panel.

Benefits

You get pixel-perfect control, instant results and total privacy. Because the tool shows the new file size and dimensions live, you always know exactly what you are downloading, and the presets remove the guesswork for common platforms.

Advantages

It is fast because there is no upload, private because nothing leaves your device, and flexible because it combines resizing with rotation, format conversion and quality control in one step. It is free and needs no signup or installation.

Supported Formats

You can load JPG, PNG, WebP, BMP, TIFF, ICO and AVIF (where the browser supports them) and export PNG, JPG, WebP, BMP and AVIF, so the resizer also works as a light format converter.

Common Use Cases

Resize a photo for an Instagram post or story, fit a YouTube thumbnail, prepare a passport photo, shrink a large camera image for email, scale artwork to A4 for printing, or make a favicon-sized icon — all in seconds.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Add one or more images by drag & drop, choosing a file, or pasting.
  2. Pick a unit and enter the new width and height, or choose a preset.
  3. Keep the aspect ratio locked to avoid stretching, or unlock it for an exact fit.
  4. Select the output format and quality, and rotate or flip if needed.
  5. Compare before and after, then download the image or all of them as a ZIP.

Best Practices

  • Keep the aspect ratio locked so images do not look stretched.
  • Enlarge sparingly — making an image bigger cannot add real detail.
  • Use WebP or JPG for photos on the web and PNG for logos and transparency.
  • For print, work in centimetres or inches at 300 DPI.

Privacy

Your images never leave your device. All resizing happens locally using the browser canvas, so you can safely resize private photos, documents or client work. Nothing is uploaded, stored or tracked.

Security

With no server and no account there is no database to breach and no credentials to leak, and the tool sets no tracking cookies. Re-encoding also removes embedded metadata such as GPS location, improving your privacy when you share the result.

Performance

Images are decoded off the main thread where the browser supports it and resized with efficient canvas operations, so even large photos process in a moment and batches stay smooth on modest devices.

Browser Compatibility

It works in Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari and Opera on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. WebP output is widely supported; AVIF output depends on the browser; and some input formats decode only where the operating system provides them.

Tips

Use percentage mode to scale by a simple factor, presets to hit platform sizes instantly, and the comparison slider to check the result before downloading. For many files, set your options once and use Resize All.

Limitations

Resizing cannot invent detail, so heavily enlarged images will look soft. Some input formats (TIFF, HEIC) and AVIF output are only available where the browser supports them, and print sizes assume a DPI you choose because most files do not store one.

Troubleshooting

If an image will not load, its format may not be supported by your browser — convert it first. If the result looks stretched, make sure the aspect ratio is locked. If a transparent PNG turns black when saved as JPG, choose a background colour before resizing.

Conclusion

Whether you are preparing a single photo or a whole folder, this online image resizer gives you exact control, instant results and complete privacy. Resize, convert and optimise your images with confidence — free and entirely in your browser.
