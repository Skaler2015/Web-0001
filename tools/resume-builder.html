ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Resume & CV Builder is a professional, privacy-first tool for creating a polished, job-ready resume directly in your browser — no design software, no account and no upload. A resume is often the single most important document in a career, and yet building one is stressful: the formatting fights you in a word processor, online builders lock the good templates behind a paywall or a login, and many of them quietly upload your personal details to a server. This tool removes all of that. You fill in a clean, guided form, a live preview assembles a beautifully formatted resume as you type, and you export it in whatever format you need — while everything stays on your own device.

The builder is organised into the sections a strong resume needs. Start with personal details — name, headline, email, phone, location, website, LinkedIn and GitHub — and, where a template allows it, a profile photo you can show as a circle or a square. Write a professional summary, then add unlimited entries for work experience (with role, company, dates and bullet-point responsibilities and achievements), education (school, degree, dates and grade), and projects (name, technologies, description and a link). Round it off with skills, soft skills, languages, certifications, hobbies, and any number of custom sections for things like awards, publications, volunteering or references. Every field updates the preview instantly, so you always see the finished result.

Presentation matters, so the tool ships with several templates. The ATS-friendly template is a clean, single-column, photo-free layout with standard headings and no decorative elements — exactly what applicant tracking systems parse most reliably. The modern template adds a tasteful accent colour, minimal keeps things understated, executive uses a refined serif for senior roles, and creative introduces a two-column layout with a sidebar. You control the accent colour and the font, and switching templates never loses your content — the same information simply re-flows into the new design. This means you can keep one ATS version for online applications and a more visual version for direct sharing, from a single set of details.

A distinctive feature is the rule-based resume score. Rather than an opaque AI judgement, it applies a transparent checklist: does the resume have a name, a valid email and phone, a summary of reasonable length, at least one experience entry with bullet points, education, a healthy number of skills, and complete contact information? Each item contributes to a score out of 100, and the panel lists exactly which sections are missing or weak so you know what to improve. Combined with live validation — email and phone formats are checked and empty required fields are flagged — it helps you produce a resume that is both complete and correctly formatted, without guesswork and without sending your data anywhere.

Export is genuinely flexible. Download a print-ready PDF to attach to applications, a PNG image for a portfolio or a message, a standalone HTML file, an editable Word (.doc) version for recruiters who ask for one, a plain TXT for copy-paste into web forms, or a JSON file that captures your entire resume as data. That JSON is also how you save and reload: import it later to continue editing, or keep it as a backup and move it between devices. On top of that, the tool auto-saves your work to the browser as you go, so if you close the tab and come back, your resume is exactly where you left it. There is no account to create and nothing to remember.

Speed and confidence come from seeing your resume take shape in real time. Because the preview is rendered from the same data on every keystroke, there is no "generate" step and no surprise when you export — what fills the page is exactly what lands in the PDF. That immediacy makes it easy to experiment: try a punchier headline, reorder your bullet points, switch from the modern template to the ATS layout for an online portal, or change the accent colour to match a company's brand, and judge each change instantly. The progress bar and score update as you go, turning a blank page into a finished, well-structured resume in a focused ten-minute session rather than an afternoon of fighting with margins.

The builder is also designed to grow with your career. A first-job fresher can keep it to a summary, education, projects and skills; a mid-career professional can stack several detailed experience entries with achievements; and a senior candidate can add custom sections for publications, patents, speaking or board roles. Because the data is structured, the same content can be presented as a compact one-pager or a fuller CV simply by choosing what to include, and the JSON export means your carefully written history is never trapped in a proprietary format — it is portable, backed up and yours to reuse whenever the next opportunity comes along.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online resume makers that require a login and upload your personal information — name, contact details, employment history — to their servers, this tool does everything locally. Your resume is assembled in the browser, saved only in your own browser storage, and exported straight to your device; nothing is transmitted, there is no account, and nothing about you is tracked or sold. That matters for a document full of personal data, and it means the tool keeps working offline once loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines and high-contrast support. Importantly, this is a genuine, deterministic builder — there are no AI features and no content is generated or sent to a model; you write your resume, and the tool formats, scores and exports exactly what you entered. In short, it is a fast, capable and genuinely private resume studio that lives entirely in your browser — professional enough for a senior CV, simple enough for a first-job fresher.

📊 ATS Guide

  • Use the ATS-friendly template for online applications — a single column parses most reliably.
  • Keep standard headings like Experience, Education and Skills so software recognises them.
  • Include a valid email and phone — the score checks both, and recruiters need them.
  • Write bullet points under each role, ideally starting with an action verb and a result.
  • List relevant skills — aim for a healthy set that matches the job description's keywords.
  • Watch the score — it flags missing sections so nothing important is left out.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • PDF looks cut off: keep the resume to one page, or reduce the summary and bullet lengths.
  • Photo not showing: some templates (ATS) hide photos by design — switch to Modern or Creative.
  • Score seems low: the panel lists missing sections — add a summary, experience bullets and skills.
  • Lost my resume: autosave keeps it on this device; export JSON regularly as a backup.
  • Word file looks plain: the .doc keeps your text and structure; fine-tune styling in your editor.
  • Need it editable elsewhere: export JSON or Word, or copy the TXT into another tool.
  • Nothing happens on export: allow downloads for the site and try a different format.
