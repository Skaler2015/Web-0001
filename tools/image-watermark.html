ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Image Watermark Adder is a professional, privacy-first editor for stamping text and logo watermarks onto your photos directly in the browser. A watermark is the simplest, most effective way to protect and brand the images you share online: it visibly marks a picture as yours, discourages unauthorised copying, and makes sure that when your work is reposted it still points back to you. Whether you are a photographer protecting a portfolio, a designer sharing mock-ups, a small business branding product shots, a blogger crediting original graphics, or simply someone who wants to sign a picture before posting it, this tool gives you fast, precise, studio-style control without any signup, subscription or upload.

At its heart is a live canvas preview that shows exactly how the final image will look as you type and adjust settings. You can add a text watermark — your name, brand, handle or a copyright line — and choose from several fonts, set the colour with a picker, and toggle bold and italic. Because text can be hard to read over busy photos, the tool includes proper legibility effects: a soft drop shadow, a contrasting outline, and a rounded background box, any of which keeps your mark visible whether it sits over a bright sky or a dark shadow. Size is expressed as a percentage of the image rather than a fixed pixel value, so the same setting looks consistent whether you watermark a small thumbnail or a full-resolution photo — which matters enormously when you process a whole batch.

Beyond text, you can stamp a logo or PNG watermark. Upload a transparent PNG, an SVG or a JPG — a studio logo, a signature graphic, a badge — and scale it to the right size; transparency is preserved so a well-made logo blends naturally onto the photo. You can even use text and logo together, for example a logo in one corner and a copyright line along the bottom. Placing the watermark is effortless: click any of the nine anchor points to snap it to a corner, edge or the centre, or simply drag it directly on the preview to any custom spot. For stronger protection you can switch from a single mark to a tiled or diagonal repeating pattern that covers the entire image, with adjustable spacing — the kind of full-page watermark used on proofs and previews so an image can be shown but not easily reused.

Every parameter is adjustable in real time. Opacity lets you go from a bold, opaque stamp to a subtle, semi-transparent mark that protects without dominating the picture. Rotation tilts the watermark to any angle, which is especially effective for diagonal, across-the-image branding. All of these controls update the preview instantly, so you are never guessing what the download will contain. When you are happy, the tool renders the watermark onto a canvas at the image's full native resolution — no downscaling, no quality loss beyond your chosen export settings — and lets you download it.

Output is flexible. You can keep the original format or convert to JPG, PNG or WebP; PNG preserves transparency and hard edges, JPG produces small files ideal for the web, and WebP offers the best size-to-quality ratio in modern browsers. A quality slider controls compression for JPG and WebP, and you can set a custom output file name so downloads are organised. Best of all, batch processing lets you drop a whole set of photos, apply identical watermark settings to every one, and download the results as a single ZIP — perfect for watermarking an event gallery, a product catalogue or a client proof set in seconds rather than one file at a time.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online watermark services that require you to upload your photos to their servers, this tool does everything locally. Your images are read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, and exported back to you — they never leave your device, there is no account or login, and nothing is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for confidential product shots, personal photos and client work alike, and it means the tool keeps working offline once the page has loaded. The interface is built to modern standards too: the dropzone and controls are keyboard-accessible and labelled for screen readers, focus outlines are visible, high-contrast preferences are respected, and the layout adapts smoothly from a spacious two-panel view on desktop to a single, touch-friendly column on phones. In short, it is a fast, private, genuinely capable watermarking studio that lives entirely in your browser — powerful enough for professional branding, simple enough for a quick copyright stamp.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🌐 Browser Compatibility

Works on all modern browsers — Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Safari, Opera and Brave — on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. It needs JavaScript, with no plugins or installation, and works offline once loaded. Canvas rendering, drag positioning and downloads are supported everywhere; WebP export is available in Chromium-based browsers and modern Firefox/Safari. Batch ZIP uses a small in-browser library that loads from a CDN.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Watermark text isn't visible: increase opacity or size, or turn on Shadow/Outline/Box for contrast.
  • Logo looks too big or small: adjust the Logo size slider — it scales relative to the image.
  • Can't drag the watermark: set the pattern to Single; tiled/diagonal cover the whole image and aren't dragged.
  • WebP option greyed out: some older browsers don't encode WebP — choose JPG or PNG.
  • Transparency lost after export: choose PNG (or WebP) as the output format; JPG has no transparency.
  • Batch ZIP didn't download: check your connection so the ZIP library can load, then try again.
  • Large photo feels slow: give it a moment; very large images take longer to render and export.
