ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Add Image to PDF tool lets you place any picture — a photograph, a company logo, a scanned signature, a rubber‑stamp graphic or a QR code — exactly where you want it on any page of a PDF, without buying desktop software or handing your documents to an online server. It behaves like the image‑placement feature of professional editors such as Adobe Acrobat, Foxit and Nitro, but it runs entirely inside your web browser, so it is instant, free and completely private. Upload a PDF, drop in an image, drag it into position, resize and style it, and download a finished PDF — the whole process takes seconds.

When you open a PDF it is rendered page by page using pdf.js, the same engine that displays PDFs inside Firefox, giving you a crisp, accurate preview to work on. Adding an image is as simple as clicking the image drop zone or dragging a file in; you can even add several images at once. Each image appears on the current page with its natural aspect ratio, ready to drag anywhere and resize from its corner handle. Aspect‑ratio lock is enabled by default so photos and logos never look squashed, and you can unlock it whenever you need to stretch an image to a specific box. A small properties panel shows the image’s name and pixel dimensions so you always know what you are working with.

Real documents need more than plain placement, so every image can be transformed and styled. Rotate it left or right in ninety‑degree steps to correct a sideways scan, flip it horizontally or vertically, dial its opacity down to create a soft watermark that sits behind the text, and add a coloured border of any thickness to frame a photo or make a stamp stand out. Alignment buttons snap an image neatly to the left, centre, right, top, middle or bottom of the page, and layer controls let you decide which image sits in front when several overlap — bring one to the front, send another to the back, or nudge it one step at a time. Any image can be duplicated to repeat it quickly.

One of the most useful capabilities is logo and watermark mode. Position an image once — say your organisation’s logo in the top corner — then choose to apply it to all pages, only odd or even pages, or a custom page range, and the tool places an identical copy at the same spot on every selected page in a single click. This turns the tedious job of branding a fifty‑page report, stamping every page of a contract, or watermarking a draft into a few seconds of work, all without any loss of quality because each copy embeds the original image data.

Working on longer documents is comfortable too. A live thumbnail strip down the side lets you jump to any page and clearly marks which pages already contain images, while page arrows, a go‑to‑page box and zoom controls help you place images precisely — zoom in to align a signature over a line, then zoom out to check the whole page. A full undo and redo history covers every action, so you can experiment freely, and a small statistics strip keeps a running count of how many images you have added and how many pages they affect. When you save, you can keep the output as a normal PDF with the images embedded as vector‑quality objects, or tick flatten to image to lock every placement permanently. The status line reports the output size and how long the export took.

The tool suits a wide range of everyday jobs. Freelancers and small businesses use it to drop a logo onto invoices, quotations and letterheads, or to stamp documents with a “Paid”, “Approved” or “Confidential” graphic. People completing forms add a scanned signature or a passport‑style photo exactly where the form asks for it, avoiding the print‑sign‑scan cycle entirely. Teachers and students insert diagrams, charts and screenshots into study material and assignments; estate agents and photographers place property or product pictures into brochures; and event organisers add sponsor logos across every page of a programme. Because placement is pixel‑precise and you can preview each page before saving, the finished document looks deliberately designed rather than hastily patched together. And since PNG transparency is respected, a logo or signature with a see‑through background sits naturally over existing text and graphics instead of hiding them behind an opaque rectangle.

Above all, this tool is private by design. Every step — rendering the PDF, decoding your images, placing them and saving the result — happens entirely on your own device using the open‑source pdf.js and pdf‑lib libraries. Nothing is uploaded to a server, nothing is stored, and no account or email is required; once the page has loaded you can even work offline. That matters because the documents and images people combine here are often sensitive — signed contracts, ID photos, letterheads, invoices and personal pictures — and they should never travel to a stranger’s server just to have a logo added. Whether you are a freelancer stamping invoices, a business branding its paperwork, a student adding a photo to a form, or anyone who needs a picture in the right place on a PDF, this tool gives you precise, professional results with nothing to install and no privacy trade‑off.

📖 How To Use

  1. Upload your PDF. Drag it into the drop zone or click to choose. It renders page by page with a thumbnail strip.
  2. Add image(s). Click the image drop zone or drag PNG/JPG files in. They appear on the current page ready to position.
  3. Position & style. Drag and resize each image, then rotate, flip, set opacity, add a border and align it precisely.
  4. Brand every page (optional). Select an image and use Apply to place it on all, odd, even or a range of pages as a logo or watermark.
  5. Save & download. Optionally tick Flatten, then click Save to download your finished PDF.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🔒 Privacy

This tool processes your PDF and images entirely inside your browser using the pdf.js and pdf‑lib libraries. Your files are never uploaded to any server, never stored, and never transmitted anywhere. No account, email or signup is required, and once the page has loaded you can even work offline. Because the images and documents combined here — signatures, ID photos, letterheads, invoices — are often personal or confidential, this local‑only design keeps them under your sole control from upload to download.

🌐 Browser Compatibility

Works in all modern browsers — Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Safari, Brave and Opera — on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. It relies only on JavaScript and the standard File, Blob and Canvas APIs plus pdf.js. Placing, dragging and resizing images works with both mouse and touch, so you can add a logo or signature from a phone or tablet as easily as from a desktop.

📄 Supported Formats

Input: a standard PDF file (.pdf) plus one or more PNG or JPG/JPEG images. PNG transparency is preserved, so logos and signatures with transparent backgrounds sit cleanly over the page. Output: a standard PDF with your images embedded — kept editable/selectable by default, or flattened to images if you choose. The result opens in any PDF viewer.
