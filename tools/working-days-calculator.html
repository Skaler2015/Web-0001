ℹ️ About This Tool

Knowing the exact number of working days between two dates is essential for project planning, calculating notice periods, tracking leave balances, setting delivery deadlines, and billing on a per-day basis. Simply counting calendar days includes weekends and holidays that you're not actually working, which leads to inaccurate estimates and missed expectations on timelines.

This calculator counts only the days that fall on genuine working days — Monday through Friday by default, or adjustable to include or exclude Saturday — and lets you optionally add specific public holidays to deduct them from the count as well. This gives you a more accurate picture of the actual time available to complete a task, project, or process within a given date range.

How to use:

  1. Select the start and end dates for the period you want to calculate.
  2. Choose your weekend configuration — Sat+Sun, Sun only, or Sat only.
  3. Optionally add any public or company holidays that fall in the range.
  4. Click "Calculate Working Days" to get the exact count.

Common uses

Project managers use this to set realistic sprint deadlines and delivery milestones. HR teams use it to calculate notice periods — a 30-day notice period means 30 calendar days, but a "one-month notice" at a company with 5-day weeks actually corresponds to roughly 22 working days. Employees use it to track remaining leave days within a period, or to confirm whether a particular task can realistically be completed within a given timeframe given the actual days available.

The holiday exclusion feature is especially valuable in India, where the number of public holidays varies significantly between states and organizations — central government employees observe different holidays from private sector employees, and regional festivals create state-specific holidays that can significantly reduce available working days in a given month. Adding these holidays ensures the calculator reflects the actual available days rather than a theoretical maximum.
