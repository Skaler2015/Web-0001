ℹ️ About This Tool

Turning a web page or any piece of raw HTML into a proper PDF is something people need surprisingly often — saving an email template as a shareable document, archiving a styled report originally built in HTML, turning a simple landing page mockup into something to print, or converting an HTML invoice or certificate template into an actual finished file you can send out right away. This tool renders your HTML exactly the way a real browser would, then captures that rendered output and carefully assembles it into a downloadable, ready-to-share PDF.

You can either paste raw HTML directly into the text box, or upload an existing .html file, and inline CSS styling (colors, fonts, layout) is respected since the tool genuinely renders the markup in a real browser context rather than just reading the text out of it. Use the Preview button first to check exactly how your content will look before converting — what you see in the preview is what ends up in the final PDF, so there's no need to download a test copy just to check your formatting.

Because this works by rendering and visually capturing your HTML, it's best suited for fairly self-contained content with inline or embedded styles. External stylesheets, custom web fonts loaded from another domain, or JavaScript-driven dynamic content may not always come through exactly as they would on a live, fully-loaded website, so for best results keep styling simple and inline where possible, and check the preview before downloading rather than assuming it will match perfectly on the first try.

The whole conversion happens locally in your browser — your HTML content is never uploaded anywhere. Once you're happy with the preview, one click produces a PDF ready to download, with no watermark, no page limit, and no account required. Long content automatically flows across as many pages as needed, so you don't have to manually figure out page breaks before converting.

If you're starting from the opposite direction — you already have a PDF and want its content as HTML instead — this site's PDF to HTML tool handles that conversion. And if your HTML references images, make sure they're either embedded as data URLs or hosted somewhere publicly accessible, since the preview and conversion both render your HTML in an isolated context that can't reach files on your local computer by a relative path alone.

How to use:

  1. Paste HTML into the box, or upload an .html file.
  2. Click Preview to check how it looks.
  3. Click Convert to PDF and download.
