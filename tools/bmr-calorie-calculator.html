Estimate your daily calorie needs based on the Mifflin-St Jeor formula.

ℹ️ About This Tool

Your Basal Metabolic Rate, or BMR, is the number of calories your body needs just to keep its basic functions running while completely at rest — breathing, circulating blood, maintaining body temperature, and supporting your organs. Total Daily Energy Expenditure, or TDEE, builds on that by adding in the calories you burn through daily activity and exercise, giving a more complete picture of how much energy your body actually uses across a typical day. This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which nutrition professionals widely consider one of the more accurate formulas for estimating BMR from basic measurements like age, height, weight, and gender.

To get your estimate, you enter your age, height, weight, and select the activity level that best matches your typical week, ranging from sedentary through to extremely active. The tool then calculates your BMR and multiplies it by an activity factor to estimate your TDEE, which represents roughly how many calories you'd need to eat daily to maintain your current weight. Alongside the maintenance figure, the tool also shows gentle, moderate calorie ranges for those with weight goals — a modest deficit range for gradual weight loss and a modest surplus range for gradual weight gain, both kept within commonly recommended, sustainable limits rather than anything extreme.

It's worth understanding that this number is an estimate based on population averages, not a precise measurement of your individual metabolism, which can vary based on muscle mass, genetics, hormonal factors, and other variables this formula doesn't capture. Two people with identical age, height, and weight can have genuinely different calorie needs in practice. This tool is meant to give you a reasonable starting point for thinking about nutrition and activity, not a precise prescription, and it deliberately avoids suggesting aggressive or extreme calorie deficits, since sustainable, gradual changes are both healthier and more likely to last.

If you're considering a meaningful change to your diet, especially anything involving a significant calorie deficit, increased training intensity, or you have an existing health condition, it's genuinely worth having that conversation with a doctor or a registered dietitian who can account for your full health picture rather than relying on a formula alone. This calculator runs entirely in your browser, with no data about your age, weight, or health ever sent anywhere, so you're free to check it privately as often as you'd like as your weight or activity level changes over time.

How to use:

  1. Select your gender, and enter your age, height, and weight.
  2. Pick the activity level that best matches your week.
  3. See your estimated maintenance calories and gentle ranges for weight goals.
