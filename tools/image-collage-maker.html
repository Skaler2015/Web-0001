ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Image Collage Maker is a professional, privacy-first tool for combining several photos into one beautifully arranged collage — directly in your browser. A collage does something a single photo cannot: it tells a whole story at a glance. A holiday becomes one frame of highlights, a product range fits in a single post, a family's year is captured on one page, and an event's best moments sit side by side. Making that used to mean fiddly design software or an online editor that uploads your private photos to a server. This tool removes all of that. It gives you flexible layouts, precise per-photo framing, tasteful borders and backgrounds, filters and a title, and it does everything locally — no installation, no signup and no upload.

The heart of the tool is a flexible layout engine. Choose Auto and it arranges your photos into a balanced grid based on how many you added; or pick an exact grid from 2 across and 2 stacked up to 3×3 and 4×4. When your photos are different shapes — some tall, some wide — the masonry layout packs them Pinterest-style into neat columns with no awkward cropping, so portrait and landscape shots live together comfortably. Every layout updates live on the canvas the instant you change it, so you can try a few and keep the one that looks best.

Because a collage is only as good as its framing, every photo is individually adjustable. Click a photo in the collage to select it, then drag to pan and use the zoom slider to decide exactly which part shows in its cell — no more faces cut off at the edge of a square crop. You can rotate and flip any single photo without affecting the others, and reset a photo's framing in one click. In the thumbnail strip you can drag to reorder your photos or remove any one of them, and add more at any time, so building and rearranging a collage feels natural and fast.

The surrounding design is fully in your hands. A spacing slider sets the gap between photos, a rounded-corners control softens each cell from sharp squares to smooth cards, and an adjustable photo border in any colour adds a framed, gallery feel. The background can be a solid colour, a two-colour gradient, or fully transparent — perfect for exporting a PNG that drops onto any design. One-tap filters (black & white, sepia, vintage, vivid, cool and warm) unify the mood across all photos so a mismatched set suddenly looks like a cohesive set, and an optional title at the top or bottom, in the colour you choose, adds a caption or a date.

Output is sized for the real world. A canvas-size menu reframes your whole collage for wherever it is going — keep the automatic square-cell layout, or switch to a square (1080×1080) post, a portrait (1080×1350), a landscape, a full story (1080×1920), a Facebook cover or a YouTube thumbnail. You can export as PNG (lossless, and transparent when you want it), JPG (small, ideal for sharing) or WebP (the best size-to-quality balance), all rendered at full resolution with a quality slider, and you can copy the collage to the clipboard or use your device's native share sheet. A full undo/redo history means you can experiment freely and step back at any time.

Speed and iteration are where a browser-based tool shines. Because the whole collage is composed on the browser's own canvas, every change — a new layout, a wider gap, a different filter, a photo nudged into a better position — appears instantly, with no waiting for a server and no page reloads. That immediacy invites experimentation: you can audition three layouts in a few seconds, try a gradient background against a solid one, or zoom into a single photo to fix a stray crop, and keep only what looks best. The undo and redo controls make that exploration risk-free, and because your settings are remembered on the device, the tool opens the way you left it. It is the kind of quick, responsive editing loop that turns a folder of loose photos into a finished, polished picture in a couple of minutes — as comfortable on a phone during a commute as on a large desktop screen.

The tool is also built to handle real photo libraries, not just perfectly matched sets. Mixed orientations, wildly different resolutions and awkward aspect ratios are common when photos come from several phones or cameras, and the layout and framing controls are designed exactly for that: masonry for shape variety, per-photo zoom and pan for framing, and cover-fit cropping so nothing is stretched or squashed. Large images are drawn efficiently and downscaled cleanly for the preview while the export keeps full resolution, so a collage of high-megapixel photos stays crisp without bogging down the editor.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online collage makers that upload your photos to a server to assemble them — or stamp a watermark on the result — this tool does everything locally. Your images are read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, combined and handed straight back to you; they never leave your device, there is no account, and nothing about your photos is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for personal family photos, private events and brand assets alike, and it keeps working offline once loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines and high-contrast support. Note that this is a genuine, deterministic layout tool — there are no AI features; every arrangement, crop and filter is a precise, repeatable operation you control, so the collage is exactly what you designed. In short, it is a fast, capable and genuinely private collage studio that lives entirely in your browser — powerful enough for a polished layout, simple enough for a quick grid.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • A photo is cropped badly: click it and use the zoom slider and drag to reframe it.
  • Photos are different shapes: use the Masonry layout so nothing is awkwardly cropped.
  • Too much empty space: reduce Spacing, or choose a layout matching your photo count.
  • Background shows through: that's the Transparent mode — switch to Solid or Gradient for a filled background.
  • Collage looks low resolution: it isn't — export is full size; the preview is just scaled to fit.
  • WebP export unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG or JPG.
  • Want a transparent PNG: set Background to Transparent and export as PNG (not JPG).
