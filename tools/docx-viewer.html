📃 DOCX Viewer

View the contents of a Word (.docx) file instantly — no Microsoft Word or Google Docs needed.

📤 Drag a .docx file here or click to choose

ℹ️ About This Tool

Opening a .docx file usually means having Microsoft Word installed, or signing into Google Docs and waiting for an upload — overkill when all you want is to quickly check what's inside a document someone sent you. This viewer renders a Word file's content directly on this page the moment you upload it, with headings, paragraphs, bold and italic text, lists, and tables all displayed properly formatted, just like they'd appear in an actual document.

This is especially useful on a phone or a computer that doesn't have Word installed, or when you just need a fast look at a document's content without committing to opening heavier software. Since everything happens locally in your browser, the file is never uploaded anywhere — it's read and displayed entirely on your own device.

How to use:

  1. Upload a .docx file.
  2. The formatted content appears immediately below.

Good to know

This tool is for viewing, not editing — if you need to make changes to the document, you'll still need Word, Google Docs, or a similar editor. It also only supports the modern .docx format used by Word 2007 and newer; the older .doc format isn't supported since it uses a completely different, more complex binary structure that can't be reliably read in a browser. If your file is in the old .doc format, opening and re-saving it as .docx in Word first will let it work here.

One of the most common situations this solves is receiving a Word document on a phone or tablet where Microsoft Word either isn't installed or is awkward to use just for a quick read. It's also useful at work or in shared computers where installing additional software isn't practical, or simply when you want to verify a document's content before deciding whether it's worth downloading the full file and opening it properly. Since the conversion happens entirely client-side using your browser's own processing, there's no waiting on a server and no concern about a sensitive document being transmitted anywhere during the viewing process.

Formatting fidelity is generally strong for everyday documents — headings keep their hierarchy, bold and italic emphasis carries over correctly, numbered and bulleted lists render properly, and tables keep their row and column structure. Things like embedded macros, tracked changes, comments, and very elaborate custom styling may not display exactly as they would in Word itself, since the viewer focuses on readable content rather than reproducing every formatting detail pixel-for-pixel.
