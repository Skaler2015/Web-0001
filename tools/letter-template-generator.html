ℹ️ About This Tool

A formal letter — whether it's a leave application, a complaint to a company, a request to a government office, or a cover letter — follows a fairly fixed structure: sender's address, date, recipient's address, a subject line, a greeting, the body, and a formal closing with a signature line. Getting the spacing and order right by hand in a word processor takes longer than it should, and it's easy to forget a section or format it inconsistently.

This tool handles the structure for you — fill in each field on the left and watch the letter build itself in a properly formatted preview on the right, styled like a traditional typed letter. Once everything looks right, Download as PDF saves it as a clean, ready-to-print or ready-to-email document.

How to use:

  1. Fill in your address, the date, and the recipient's address.
  2. Add a subject line, greeting, the main body of your letter, and a closing.
  3. Check the live preview on the right, then click "Download as PDF."

Tips for a stronger formal letter

Keep the subject line short and specific — it should tell the reader exactly what the letter is about before they read a single sentence of the body. In the body itself, state the purpose of the letter in the first sentence or two rather than building up to it, since formal letters are read for information, not suspense. Closing phrases like "Yours sincerely" are typically paired with a known recipient name, while "Yours faithfully" is more traditional when the letter opens with "Dear Sir/Madam" and the recipient's exact name isn't known.

This kind of letter comes up more often than people expect — applying for leave at work or school, formally requesting a document from a government office, writing to a landlord about a repair, lodging a complaint with a company's customer service department, or sending a cover letter alongside a job application. In many of these situations, especially with government and institutional offices, a properly formatted physical or PDF letter is taken more seriously than an informal email, which is exactly the gap this template is built to fill without requiring any design or word-processing skill, letting anyone produce a properly structured letter in a few minutes regardless of how often they write one, without having to remember the right spacing, salutation style, or sign-off convention each time from scratch.
