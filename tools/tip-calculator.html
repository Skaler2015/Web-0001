ℹ️ About This Tool

The Professional Bill Splitting & Tip Calculator is a free, browser-based tool that settles the whole "who owes what" question in one place. It grew out of a basic tip calculator into a complete bill manager because real bills are rarely just an amount and a tip: they carry taxes such as GST or VAT, sometimes a service charge, occasionally a discount or coupon, and they usually have to be divided among people who did not all order the same thing. This tool models every one of those elements accurately and then splits the result fairly, giving each person a clean number and an easy way to pay or be reimbursed — without any of the mental arithmetic that turns the end of a good meal into an awkward negotiation.

You begin with the bill details: the subtotal, and optionally the restaurant name, a bill number, the date and your currency. Eight currencies are supported — INR, USD, EUR, GBP, AED, SGD, AUD and CAD — each formatted with the correct local grouping so the figures look right wherever you are. Next comes the tip, which is more flexible than a single percentage box. You can choose a preset from 5% up to 25%, type any custom percentage, switch to a fixed tip amount, ask the tool to simply round the total up to a neat figure, or turn the tip off entirely. Crucially, you can decide whether the tip is calculated on the pre-tax subtotal or on the post-tax total, because conventions differ and that choice can change the tip noticeably on a taxed bill.

Taxes and charges are handled properly rather than lumped into a guess. You pick a tax type — GST, CGST + SGST, IGST, VAT, sales tax or a custom rate — and the tool applies it to the taxable amount; for CGST + SGST it shows the split halves the way an Indian restaurant bill does. A separate service-charge percentage sits alongside it, and a discount (flat amount or percentage, with an optional coupon label) is applied before tax, exactly as it would be on a genuine receipt. The order of operations matters for accuracy: discount first, then tax and service on the discounted amount, then tip, then the grand total. Getting that sequence right is one of the main reasons a dedicated tool beats doing it in your head.

Splitting is where the tool really earns its place. The simplest mode divides the grand total equally among the number of people you set, with quick "Couple" and "Family" buttons for common group sizes. When the split is not even, you have three fairer options. Shares lets you weight each person — useful when some people had more, or when a couple counts as one paying unit. Custom amounts lets you type exactly what each person will pay and shows whether the entered amounts add up to the total. And item-wise splitting lets you enter each dish with its price and tick who shared it; the tool then charges every person for their items and distributes the tax, service charge and tip in proportion to what they ordered — the fairest possible split, and the one that ends most disagreements. Every person can be named and renamed, so the final summary reads like a real settlement rather than "Person 1, Person 2".

Once the split is calculated, rounding keeps the payments practical. You can round each person's share up or down, or to the nearest 1, 5, 10 or 50 units of your currency, so nobody is chasing small change. The full breakdown — subtotal, discount, tax, service charge, tip, grand total and each person's share — is shown in a clear summary and can be turned into a printable receipt with the restaurant name and date. From there, sharing is a single tap: copy the summary to your clipboard, send it straight to a WhatsApp chat, open a pre-filled email, or display a QR code that a friend can scan to read the breakdown. For records or reimbursement, the same data exports as CSV, an Excel file, a PDF or a PNG image.

Everything is built to be quick, private and dependable. The calculation runs live as you type, with no "calculate" button to hunt for, and because it is entirely client-side it keeps working on flaky restaurant Wi-Fi or fully offline once loaded. Your bills, names and amounts are never uploaded; recent and starred bills are saved only in your own browser for quick recall. Inputs are validated to catch empty, negative or nonsensical values before they produce a wrong split. The layout is responsive and touch-friendly for use on a phone at the table, and the controls are labelled and keyboard-accessible. It is a small tool by ambition but a thorough one in execution — designed so that restaurants, cafés, hotels, offices, families, travellers, businesses and students can all reach a fair, agreed number in seconds.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Per-person total looks off: check the tip-on setting (pre-tax vs post-tax) and whether a service charge or discount is applied.
  • Custom amounts don't balance: the summary shows the remaining difference — adjust one person's amount until it reaches zero.
  • Item-wise totals seem wrong: make sure every item is assigned to at least one person; unassigned items are not charged to anyone.
  • The QR button did nothing: the QR module may be blocked offline — use Copy or WhatsApp instead, which need no external code.
  • Currency symbol looks wrong: pick your currency at the top; formatting follows that currency's locale.
  • Export didn't download: allow downloads for this site in your browser and try again.
