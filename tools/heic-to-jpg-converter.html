ℹ️ About This Tool

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is the default photo format on iPhones since iOS 11 — it produces smaller files than JPG at similar quality, which is great for saving storage on the phone, but it causes a common headache the moment you try to use those photos anywhere else: most Windows programs, websites, and Android apps simply can't open a .heic file at all, showing a broken image icon or an outright error. The usual fix is changing a setting deep in the iPhone's camera settings before taking the photo, which doesn't help with photos you've already taken.

This tool decodes HEIC files directly in your browser and converts them to JPG or PNG, the two formats every device, website, and program can open without question. You can convert several photos at once — drop in a whole folder's worth, choose JPG or PNG, and download each result individually or grab everything together as a single ZIP file. Because the conversion happens locally using your browser's own processing power, your photos are never sent to any server in the process.

How to use:

  1. Choose whether you want JPG or PNG as the output format.
  2. Upload one or more HEIC/HEIF photos.
  3. Download each converted photo individually, or click Download All as ZIP.

JPG or PNG — which should you pick?

JPG is the better choice for regular photos — it keeps file sizes small while looking essentially identical to the original for everyday pictures, and it's universally accepted everywhere photos are uploaded. PNG is a better choice only in the rare case where the HEIC image actually needs transparency preserved or you specifically want a lossless copy with zero further compression artifacts, though PNG files end up noticeably larger. For typical iPhone camera photos, JPG is almost always the right pick.

This also solves the common situation of receiving HEIC photos from someone else — over WhatsApp, email, or AirDrop from an iPhone user — and not being able to open them on a Windows PC or an older Android phone. Rather than asking the sender to re-export or change their camera settings, you can simply drop the received HEIC files in here and get back standard JPG or PNG files that open everywhere without any further hassle, fuss, or need to install separate desktop software just to view a handful of photos someone sent you.
