ℹ️ About This Tool

Compound interest is often described as one of the most powerful forces in personal finance, and the reason is simple: unlike simple interest, which only ever earns on your original principal, compound interest earns on your principal plus all the interest it has already accumulated, so your money grows faster the longer it stays invested. Calculating this by hand requires raising a number to a power based on how many compounding periods have passed, which gets genuinely unwieldy once you're dealing with monthly or daily compounding over several years — exactly the kind of calculation this tool is built to handle instantly.

You enter your principal amount, the annual interest rate, how many years you plan to let it grow, and how often the interest compounds — annually, semi-annually, quarterly, monthly, or daily. More frequent compounding means slightly faster growth for the same nominal rate, since interest starts earning interest sooner. The tool also lets you add an optional monthly contribution on top of your initial principal, which is realistic for most people who aren't just parking a lump sum but also adding to their savings or investment regularly.

The result breaks down your total invested amount versus the interest earned purely from growth, plus a year-by-year bar chart so you can visually see how the curve steepens over time — in the early years, growth looks almost linear, but by the later years the compounding effect becomes dramatically visible, often contributing more to the final total than all your contributions combined. This visual makes a genuinely abstract concept much easier to internalize than just looking at a single final number.

This calculator is useful for comparing different savings or investment options, planning how long you'd need to leave money invested to hit a target amount, or simply satisfying curiosity about how a fixed deposit, recurring deposit, or investment account might grow over time. Keep in mind that this assumes a constant rate of return throughout, which real-world fixed deposits, savings accounts, and investments rarely guarantee for very long periods — actual rates can change, and market-linked investments fluctuate. Use this as a planning estimate, and verify current rates with your bank or financial institution before making decisions. As with the rest of this site, everything is calculated directly in your browser — you can experiment with different principal amounts, rates, tenures, and contribution levels as many times as you like, with nothing ever stored or sent anywhere, making it easy to compare several what-if scenarios side by side before settling on a savings or investment plan.

How to use:

  1. Enter your principal amount, interest rate, and duration.
  2. Pick a compounding frequency.
  3. Optionally add a monthly contribution to see how regular top-ups boost your final amount.
