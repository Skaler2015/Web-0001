ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware PDF Compare tool lets you place two versions of a PDF side by side and see, in an instant, exactly what text was added, removed or changed between them. Whenever a document goes through revisions — a contract that has been renegotiated, a policy that has been updated, a report edited by several people, a manuscript returned by a proofreader — the hardest and most error‑prone job is spotting every small change. Reading both files line by line is slow and unreliable, and a single altered figure, deleted clause or reworded sentence is easy to miss. This tool does that comparison automatically and shows the result in clear colour: green for what is new, red for what is gone.

Under the hood it uses two trusted open‑source libraries. pdf.js, the same rendering engine that powers Firefox’s built‑in PDF viewer, extracts the selectable text from every page of each document. Then jsdiff, a mature and widely‑used difference engine, computes precisely how the two texts differ. Because both run inside your browser, comparison is fast and — crucially — completely private: your documents are never sent to a server. The result is a professional‑grade text comparison of the kind normally found only in paid desktop software such as Adobe Acrobat Pro, Foxit, PDF‑XChange Editor or the classic DiffPDF utility, available here free and with nothing to install.

You are in full control of how the comparison is performed. A comparison‑level selector lets you diff word by word (the default and most readable), character by character for the finest possible detail, line by line for a structural overview, or sentence by sentence for prose. Three ignore options further refine the result: ignore case treats “Total” and “total” as identical, ignore extra spaces collapses differences in indentation and spacing that don’t affect meaning, and ignore punctuation hides changes to commas, full stops and brackets. Together these let you filter out cosmetic noise and concentrate on genuine content changes.

The results are presented in three complementary views. The inline view weaves additions and deletions into a single flowing document, which is ideal for reading the change in context. The side‑by‑side view places the original on the left and the updated version on the right, each with its own highlighting, mirroring the classic two‑pane redline layout that lawyers and editors expect. The change list view distills everything down to a compact, scrollable list of just the differences, so you can audit them one by one. A statistics strip reports the number of words added, words removed, sections changed and an overall similarity percentage, giving you an immediate sense of how much the document really moved.

Navigating a long comparison is effortless. Next and previous buttons jump straight from one change to the next, highlighting the current difference so you never lose your place, while filters let you show only additions, only removals, or hide unchanged text entirely to focus on what matters. Two small PDF information panels summarise each file — name, size, page count, PDF version, author, creator and creation and modification dates — so you always know exactly which two versions are on screen. When you are done, you can export the differences as a plain‑text report for a quick change log, a self‑contained HTML report that preserves the colour highlighting for sharing, or a CSV file that opens neatly in Excel for formal review and record‑keeping — each report also records the two file names and a timestamp as an executive summary.

The tool fits naturally into the everyday work of many roles. Lawyers and paralegals use it to redline successive drafts of contracts, NDAs and settlement agreements, catching a quietly altered clause or a changed number before signing. Editors, authors and academics compare manuscript drafts, tracking a proofreader’s revisions and confirming that requested corrections were actually applied. Procurement and finance teams compare tender responses, invoices and financial statements between revisions to verify figures. Compliance and quality officers confirm that a published policy, standard operating procedure or datasheet matches the approved version and that no unauthorised edits crept in. Even for everyday tasks — checking which copy of a résumé, application form or price list is the newest — it removes the guesswork. Because the comparison is text‑based and language‑agnostic, it works equally well on English, Hindi and mixed‑script documents, so long as the PDF contains a genuine, selectable text layer rather than a flat scanned image.

Above all, PDF Compare is private by design. Every step — reading the PDFs, extracting their text and computing the differences — happens on your own device. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is stored, and no account or email is required; once the page has loaded you can even work offline. That matters enormously for the documents people most often need to compare, because contracts, tenders, financial statements, legal filings and internal reports are precisely the material that must never be exposed to a third‑party server. Whether you are a lawyer redlining an agreement, an editor tracking manuscript changes, a compliance officer verifying that only approved edits were made, or simply someone checking which version of a form is the latest, this tool turns a tedious, error‑prone manual check into a fast, reliable and confidential one.

अंदरूनी तौर पर यह दो भरोसेमंद ओपन‑सोर्स लाइब्रेरी का उपयोग करता है। pdf.js — वही इंजन जो Firefox के अंतर्निर्मित PDF व्यूअर को चलाता है — हर पेज से चयन‑योग्य टेक्स्ट निकालता है। फिर jsdiff, एक परिपक्व और व्यापक रूप से उपयोग किया जाने वाला डिफ़रेंस इंजन, सटीक रूप से गणना करता है कि दोनों टेक्स्ट कैसे भिन्न हैं। चूँकि दोनों आपके ब्राउज़र के अंदर चलते हैं, तुलना तेज़ और पूरी तरह निजी होती है: आपके दस्तावेज़ कभी सर्वर पर नहीं भेजे जाते। परिणाम पेशेवर‑स्तर की टेक्स्ट तुलना है, जो सामान्यतः Adobe Acrobat Pro, Foxit या DiffPDF जैसे भुगतान वाले सॉफ़्टवेयर में ही मिलती है — यहाँ मुफ़्त और बिना कुछ इंस्टॉल किए।

तुलना कैसे हो, इस पर आपका पूरा नियंत्रण है। एक तुलना‑स्तर चयनकर्ता आपको शब्द‑दर‑शब्द (डिफ़ॉल्ट और सबसे पठनीय), अक्षर‑दर‑अक्षर बारीकी के लिए, पंक्ति‑दर‑पंक्ति संरचनात्मक अवलोकन के लिए, या वाक्य‑दर‑वाक्य गद्य के लिए तुलना करने देता है। तीन ignore विकल्प परिणाम को और परिष्कृत करते हैं: ignore case “Total” और “total” को समान मानता है, ignore extra spaces इंडेंटेशन और रिक्ति के अंतर को मिटाता है, और ignore punctuation अल्पविराम, पूर्णविराम और कोष्ठकों के बदलावों को छिपाता है। ये मिलकर आपको केवल असली सामग्री बदलावों पर ध्यान देने देते हैं।

परिणाम तीन पूरक व्यू में दिखते हैं। Inline व्यू जोड़ और हटाव को एक बहते दस्तावेज़ में बुनता है, जो बदलाव को संदर्भ में पढ़ने के लिए आदर्श है। Side‑by‑side व्यू मूल को बाईं ओर और अपडेटेड को दाईं ओर रखता है, हर एक की अपनी हाइलाइटिंग के साथ — वही क्लासिक दो‑पैनल रेडलाइन लेआउट जिसकी वकील और संपादक अपेक्षा करते हैं। Change list व्यू सब कुछ केवल बदलावों की एक संक्षिप्त सूची में समेट देता है। एक आँकड़ा पट्टी जोड़े गए शब्द, हटाए गए शब्द, बदले गए खंड और कुल समानता प्रतिशत बताती है।

लंबी तुलना में नेविगेट करना आसान है। Next और Previous बटन सीधे एक बदलाव से अगले तक ले जाते हैं और वर्तमान अंतर को हाइलाइट करते हैं, जबकि फ़िल्टर आपको केवल जोड़, केवल हटाव दिखाने, या अपरिवर्तित टेक्स्ट पूरी तरह छिपाने देते हैं। दो छोटे PDF जानकारी पैनल हर फ़ाइल का सार देते हैं — नाम, आकार, पेज संख्या, PDF वर्शन, लेखक, निर्माता और तिथियाँ। काम पूरा होने पर आप अंतरों को एक सादा‑टेक्स्ट रिपोर्ट, रंग‑हाइलाइटिंग सुरक्षित रखने वाली स्वतंत्र HTML रिपोर्ट, या Excel में साफ़ खुलने वाली CSV फ़ाइल के रूप में निर्यात कर सकते हैं — हर रिपोर्ट में दोनों फ़ाइल नाम और टाइमस्टैम्प भी दर्ज होता है।

📖 How To Use

  1. Upload both PDFs. Add the original on the left and the updated version on the right. Each file’s details load automatically; use ⇄ to swap them.
  2. Choose options. Pick the comparison level — word, character, line or sentence — and toggle ignore case, extra spaces or punctuation.
  3. Compare. Click Compare Documents. Added text appears green, removed text red, with counts and a similarity score.
  4. Review. Switch between inline, side‑by‑side and change‑list views, jump between differences, and filter to show only what changed.
  5. Export. Download a TXT, HTML or CSV report, or copy the changes to share with your team.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🔒 Privacy

This tool compares your documents entirely inside your browser using the pdf.js and jsdiff libraries. Your PDF files are never uploaded to any server, never stored, and never transmitted anywhere. No account, email or signup is required, and once the page has loaded you can even work offline. Because the documents people compare — contracts, tenders, financial statements and legal filings — contain highly sensitive information, this local‑only design keeps both versions under your sole control at all times.

🌐 Browser Compatibility

Works in all modern browsers — Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Safari, Brave and Opera — on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. It relies only on JavaScript and the standard File and Blob APIs plus pdf.js, which are widely supported. On phones and tablets the layout stacks into a single column and the side‑by‑side panes scroll independently for comfortable review on smaller screens.

📄 Supported Formats

Input: two standard PDF files (.pdf) that contain selectable, machine‑readable text. Purely scanned or image‑only PDFs have no extractable text, so run them through an OCR tool first. Output: an on‑screen colour comparison plus optional TXT, HTML and CSV difference reports. This tool compares the text of two PDFs; it does not alter either original file.
