ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware QR Code Generator is a professional, privacy-first tool for turning almost any piece of information into a scannable QR code — entirely inside your browser. Where a basic generator only accepts a link and a size, this tool understands more than a dozen QR types: plain website URLs, free text, email messages, SMS, phone numbers, WhatsApp chats, WiFi network access, full contact cards (vCard), calendar events, map locations and — importantly for India — UPI payment codes that work with PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm and every other UPI app. Each type shows a purpose-built form, so you never have to remember the exact text format a QR expects; you simply fill in the fields and the correct, standards-compliant payload is built for you and encoded live.

A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that stores data in a grid of black and white squares called modules. A scanner reads the pattern and recovers the text — a link, a WiFi password, a payment request — instantly. What makes QR codes so robust is error correction: extra redundant data is woven into the code so it still scans even if part of it is dirty, damaged or covered by a logo. This generator lets you pick the error-correction level (L, M, Q or H, from about 7% up to 30% recovery), which is the key setting when you add a centre logo: the higher the level, the more of the code can be obscured while remaining readable. All of this encoding happens with a well-tested QR algorithm running locally, and the visual rendering — colours, shapes, logo and margins — is drawn by the tool itself, giving you precise control over the final image.

Customisation is where this generator goes far beyond the basics. You can set any foreground and background colour (or make the background fully transparent for overlaying on other designs), adjust the quiet-zone margin, and choose module and eye styles — classic squares, softly rounded modules, or circular dots — to give the code a modern look. You can upload a logo and control its size and whether it sits on a clean white pad, and add a short caption beneath the code such as “Scan to pay” or “Join our WiFi”. Every change is reflected instantly in the live preview so you can dial in exactly the look you want. When you are happy, download the result as a high-resolution PNG, a lossy JPG or WEBP, a crisp scalable SVG vector for print, or a ready-to-share PDF — or copy the image straight to your clipboard, print it, or share it with your device's share sheet.

For anyone who needs many codes at once, the built-in batch generator turns a list of links or values — typed one per line or imported from a CSV file — into a gallery of QR codes you can download together as a ZIP archive. This is ideal for event tickets, product labels, table-ordering codes, asset tags or a set of personalised links, and it saves the tedium of generating each one by hand. A local history keeps your recent codes within reach and you can favourite the ones you reuse, all stored only in your browser. A one-click reset returns everything to defaults whenever you want to start fresh.

Privacy and security are fundamental to the design. Every QR code is generated on your own device using JavaScript; the link, WiFi password, contact details or payment information you encode is never uploaded to any server, and there is no account, login or tracking. This matters a great deal for QR codes specifically, because they often contain sensitive data — a WiFi password, a home address on a vCard, or payment details — that you would never want passing through a third-party service. Because the tool is fully client-side, it is also fast (codes appear the moment you type) and continues to work with no internet connection once the page has loaded. The only thing downloaded from the network is the small, open-source QR encoding library; your data stays with you.

Finally, the tool is built to modern standards of accessibility, responsiveness and maintainability. Controls are keyboard-navigable and labelled for screen readers, focus outlines are visible, and high-contrast preferences are respected. The two-column layout collapses gracefully to a single column on tablets and phones, with touch-friendly inputs and a preview that scales to the screen. Internally, the QR types and customisation options are defined as small, readable configuration objects, so new types or styles can be added without disturbing the core generation logic — an important quality for a tool that businesses may rely on for payments, marketing and operations. In short, it is a fast, private, richly featured QR generator suitable for casual use and for a commercial QR platform alike.

🎨 Customization Options

  • Size — 200 to 1000 px for the raster export (SVG/PDF scale to any size).
  • Error correction — L (7%), M (15%), Q (25%) or H (30%); use H when adding a logo.
  • Margin — the quiet zone around the code (0–10 modules).
  • Colours — any foreground and background colour, or a transparent background.
  • Module & eye style — square, rounded or dots, with square, rounded or circular finder eyes.
  • Logo — upload an image, set its size and optional white padding (keep it small and use ECC H).
  • Caption — a short label printed beneath the code.
⚠️ Scannability tip: keep good contrast (dark foreground on light background), avoid inverting colours, keep the logo under ~25% of the code, and always test the printed QR with a phone before mass printing.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • QR won't scan: increase error correction (Q or H), raise the contrast, reduce the logo size, or add more margin.
  • Preview is blank: a required field is empty — fill in the form for the selected type.
  • “Data too long”: QR codes have a capacity limit — shorten the text or use a URL that points to the content.
  • Logo covers too much: keep the logo under ~25% and use error correction H.
  • Copy QR did nothing: some browsers block clipboard image writing — download the PNG instead.
  • UPI QR not paying: check the UPI ID (VPA) and amount; some apps require the payee name to be filled.
