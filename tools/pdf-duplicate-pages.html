ℹ️ About This Tool

Sometimes you genuinely need a single page to appear twice within one PDF — a blank page inserted for double-sided printing alignment, a divider page repeated between sections, a terms-and-conditions page that needs to show up in two places, or simply a template page you want to fill in multiple times within the same document. This tool lets you pick any page, or several at once, and insert exact copies of each one exactly where you want them, without retyping or rebuilding anything by hand.

After uploading your PDF, every page appears as its own clickable thumbnail in a responsive grid (or list). Click any page to mark it, choose how many copies to make and where to place them — after the original, before it, at the very start or end, or at a custom page number — and watch the live output preview show the exact final page order before you commit. Ranges like 1,3,8-15, odd/even selection, select-all, invert and undo/redo make even large jobs quick.

This comes up more often than people expect with real paperwork: printers that need a blank separator page between double-sided sections, contracts where a signature page needs to repeat for multiple signatories, or worksheets and forms where the same page needs to be filled out more than once before scanning everything back together as one file.

The entire process runs locally in your browser using PDF.js and PDF-lib — your document is never uploaded anywhere just to copy a page. Thumbnails are rendered only as they scroll into view and cached, so the tool stays fast even for very large PDFs. Once finished, the new PDF downloads immediately, with no watermark, no page limit, and no account required.

It's worth distinguishing this from simply copying the whole file — you're working within a single document, so the duplicated page keeps its exact position relative to everything around it, which matters when page order and adjacency carry meaning. If you ever need the opposite operation, removing pages instead of adding copies, the PDF Delete Pages tool on this site uses the exact same thumbnail-based selection approach.

How to use:

  1. Upload your PDF.
  2. Click the pages to duplicate (or use range / odd / even / all).
  3. Pick the copy count and insert position, check the preview.
  4. Click duplicate and download the new PDF.
