ℹ️ About This Tool

PDF to JPG / PNG Converter (English)

The PDF to JPG/PNG Converter by ApneSoftware turns the pages of a PDF into ready-to-use image files — JPG, PNG, WEBP, BMP, TIFF or AVIF — right inside your web browser. Whether you need one page as a picture for an online form, a diagram lifted out of a report, or an entire scanned document exported as images, this tool renders each page crisply at the resolution you choose and lets you download pages one by one or all together in a ZIP, without ever uploading your file to a server.

Introduction

PDFs are perfect for sharing finished documents, but many websites, forms and apps only accept images. Government and school portals, older content systems, social platforms and countless upload forms want a JPG or PNG, not a PDF. Taking a screenshot gives you a blurry, badly-cropped result; this converter instead renders the real page content at a precise DPI, so the image is sharp, correctly sized and true to the original. It is the fast, reliable way to move from document format to image format whenever you need to.

Main Features

  • Six output formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP, BMP, TIFF and AVIF (where your browser supports it).
  • Full page selection: all pages, custom ranges (like 1-5,8), odd, even, first, last, or individual checkboxes.
  • Batch & multi-file: add several PDFs at once and convert them together.
  • Quality control: DPI from 72 to 600, quality presets, a JPEG/WEBP quality slider and compression levels.
  • Resize options: keep the original DPI-based size or fit to a target width, height or maximum side.
  • Page management: rotate, delete and drag-to-reorder pages before converting.
  • Preview: thumbnails plus a full-screen preview with zoom in and out.
  • Output: download pages individually, download only the selected pages, or grab everything as a ZIP, with custom file naming.
  • Password-protected PDFs: enter the password when prompted and convert as usual.
  • Convenience: progress bar with estimated time, pause, resume and cancel, plus a dark/light theme toggle and keyboard shortcuts.

Benefits

The tool saves you from clumsy workarounds. Instead of screenshotting pages or installing heavy software, you get precise, high-quality images in seconds. Because it runs entirely on your device, it is fast even on a slow connection, works offline once loaded, and keeps confidential documents private. It is completely free, with no watermark, no sign-up and no limit on the number of pages or files.

Why Use This Tool

Many online converters upload your PDF to a remote server, cap the number of pages, or stamp a watermark on the output. This one does none of that. It offers more formats and finer control — DPI, quality, resize, rotation, page selection and ZIP export — than most paid alternatives, in a clean, modern interface that works equally well on desktop and mobile.

Common Use Cases

  • Uploading a single PDF page to a form that only accepts JPG or PNG.
  • Pulling a chart, diagram or figure out of a report for a slide deck.
  • Turning a scanned multi-page document into a set of images.
  • Creating high-resolution images of pages for printing or zooming into fine detail.
  • Sharing a page as a quick image on chat or social media where PDFs do not preview.
  • Exporting pages as WEBP or AVIF to save space on a website.

Privacy & Security

Privacy is central to the design. Your PDF is read with the browser's File API and rendered with a client-side PDF engine; nothing is transmitted, cached on a server or retained after you close the tab. Password-protected files are handled locally too — the password you type is used only in your browser to unlock the document for rendering. This makes the tool suitable for personal, business, legal, financial and medical documents that should never pass through a third-party server.

Supported Formats & Browser Compatibility

Input is any standard PDF. Output covers JPG, PNG, WEBP, BMP and TIFF everywhere, with AVIF where the browser can encode it. The converter works in the latest Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Opera and Brave on Windows, macOS, Linux, Android and iOS. A modern browser with JavaScript is all you need — nothing to install.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use 300 DPI for printing and 96–150 DPI for on-screen use to keep files small.
  • Choose PNG for text and line art, and JPG or WEBP for photos and scans.
  • Convert only the pages you need with the selection tools to save time and memory.
  • Rotate any sideways pages before converting so the images come out upright.
  • Use the ZIP download for large documents instead of many separate files.

Limitations

Very high DPI on large documents uses more memory, so extremely long PDFs at 600 DPI may be slow on low-end devices — reduce the DPI or convert in smaller batches. AVIF and, on some browsers, WEBP encoding depend on the browser; if a format is unavailable the tool will fall back gracefully. The converter renders pages as images, so the output is not editable text unless you use a dedicated OCR tool afterwards.

Conclusion

This PDF to JPG/PNG converter combines a capable rendering engine with rich, professional controls and a strict privacy model — all free and entirely in the browser. From a single quick export to a full batch of high-resolution images, it gives you exactly the pages you need, in the format and quality you choose, without your files ever leaving your device.
