ℹ️ About This Tool

Plain text files are simple and universal, but they're not always the right format to share or print — a PDF looks more finished, keeps its page breaks and layout fixed no matter what device opens it, and is the expected format for printed handouts, simple reports, or notes you want to archive in a consistent way. This tool turns any .txt file, or text you paste directly, into a properly paginated PDF with sensible margins and word wrapping, ready to share or print.

You can either upload an existing .txt file or just type or paste text straight into the box — both approaches end up producing exactly the same kind of result. Choose a page size (A4 is standard in most of the world, Letter is standard in the US and a few other countries) and a font size that suits your content, and the tool automatically wraps your text and splits it across as many pages as needed, just like a word processor would, without you having to manually insert any page breaks yourself.

This is useful for turning quick notes, code snippets, exported chat logs, scraped text, or anything else that started life as plain text into something presentable enough to email, print, or file away, especially when the recipient expects a proper document rather than a raw .txt attachment. It's also a fast way to create a basic PDF without opening a full word processor just for a few short paragraphs of plain content.

The whole conversion happens locally in your browser using a PDF-generation library — your text is never uploaded anywhere. Once converted, the PDF downloads immediately, with no watermark, no page limit, and no account required. Whether you're converting a two-line note or a multi-page document full of text, the same word-wrapping and pagination logic applies automatically, so you never have to manually check where one page ends and the next begins.

One detail worth knowing: this tool uses a standard, widely compatible font rather than trying to preserve any custom font your original text editor might have used, since plain text files don't carry font information at all. If you need rich formatting like bold text, multiple fonts, or embedded images alongside your content, this tool intentionally stays simple and text-only — for richer documents, the site's Word to PDF tool is a better fit if your source content already has formatting applied.

How to use:

  1. Upload a .txt file or paste your text into the box.
  2. Choose your page size and font size.
  3. Click Convert and download the PDF.
