ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Batch Image Compressor & Resizer is a professional, privacy-first studio for processing many images at once — compressing, resizing, converting, transforming and watermarking a whole batch and downloading the results as a single ZIP, all directly in your browser. Anyone who works with images in volume knows the pain of doing it one at a time: a folder of holiday photos to shrink for email, a product catalogue to resize for a shop, a set of screenshots to compress for a blog, or a hundred assets to convert to WebP for a faster website. Doing that individually is tedious, and most "bulk" websites make you upload your private images to a server first. This tool removes both problems: it handles the entire batch in one workflow, and it does everything locally so your images never leave your device.

The heart of the studio is an unlimited queue. Add images by dragging a selection in, clicking to choose, adding an entire folder, or pasting from the clipboard, and each appears as a row with a thumbnail, its name and its original size. You can drag rows to reorder the queue, remove any single image, or clear the whole list, and duplicate detection quietly skips a file you have already added. There is no artificial limit on how many images you can queue — the tool is built to handle a large batch, processing files one after another so your browser stays responsive and a progress bar keeps you informed.

Resizing is flexible enough for any target. Choose max dimensions to cap width and/or height while preserving the aspect ratio — perfect for making sure nothing exceeds, say, 1920 pixels — or percentage scaling to shrink everything to a proportion of its original size. When you need an exact output, exact size lets you set precise width and height with a cover (crop to fill) or contain (fit inside) option, and keep original leaves dimensions untouched when you only want to compress or convert. Because every image in the batch is measured individually, portrait and landscape photos are each fitted correctly to your rule.

Compression and conversion put you in control of the trade-off between size and quality. Export as JPG (smallest, ideal for photos), PNG (lossless, with transparency) or WebP (the best size-to-quality balance in modern browsers), and tune the quality with a slider or the quick High / Medium / Low presets. Converting a batch of PNG screenshots to JPG or WebP, for example, can cut total size dramatically. On top of sizing and format, the studio adds genuine batch operations: rotate and flip to fix orientation, brightness, saturation and one-tap filters for a consistent look, a text watermark with position control to brand every image, and a rename prefix so the output files follow your naming scheme. Every setting applies uniformly to the whole batch, which is exactly what makes bulk processing worthwhile.

What sets this apart from a blind bulk tool is visibility. A live preview shows the first image processed with your current settings, and an estimated size, so you can dial things in before committing. After processing, each row reports its original size, new size and percentage saved, and a statistics panel totals the whole batch — how many images, the combined original and new sizes, and the overall saving. You can download a single file straight from its row, or use the big Compress & Download All button to get every processed image in one ZIP, named per your prefix. There is also a process-without-download option so you can review the results and totals first.

Performance and workflow are treated as carefully as the output. Each image is decoded, drawn to a canvas, resized, compressed and re-encoded on the browser's own graphics pipeline, and the queue is worked through one file at a time with a live progress bar so the interface never freezes — even on a large batch of high-megapixel photos. Because the preview and the final export share the same processing code, what you see in the live preview is exactly what lands in the ZIP, which removes the guesswork that plagues blind bulk tools. Settings persist on your device between sessions, so a workflow you use often — say, "resize to 1600 px, WebP, 75%" for a website — is one tap away every time. The whole loop, from dropping in a folder to downloading the archive, is designed to feel fast and predictable whether you are on a laptop or a phone.

The studio is deliberately built for the variety of real image libraries. Photos arrive in different orientations, resolutions and formats, and the per-image measurement, the rotate and flip controls, and the fit options exist so a mixed set comes out consistent rather than chaotic. Duplicate detection stops the same file sneaking in twice, drag-sorting lets you control the order files appear in the archive, and the rename prefix keeps a batch tidy and easy to find later. Nothing about the pipeline is hidden: the same deterministic maths runs on every image, so the results are transparent and repeatable, and you can always re-run a batch with a tweaked setting and compare the savings.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online batch compressors that upload your images to a server — often dozens of private photos at a time — this tool does everything locally. Each image is read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, resized, compressed and re-encoded, and the ZIP is assembled in the browser too; nothing leaves your device, there is no account, and nothing about your images is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for personal photos, client deliverables and confidential assets alike, and it keeps working offline once loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines and high-contrast support. Note that this is a genuine, deterministic processing tool — there are no AI features; every resize, compression and transform is a precise, repeatable operation you control, so the output is exactly what you configured. In short, it is a fast, capable and genuinely private batch studio that lives entirely in your browser — powerful enough for a full content pipeline, simple enough for a quick folder cleanup.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Files didn't get smaller: lower the quality, choose WebP, or resize down with max dimensions.
  • PNG output is large: PNG is lossless — switch to JPG or WebP for photos.
  • Image looks cropped: in Exact size, switch Fit from Cover to Contain.
  • Transparency turned black/white: exporting to JPG has no transparency — use PNG or WebP.
  • Big batch is slow: very large images take longer; resize down or process in smaller groups.
  • WebP unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG or JPG.
  • ZIP didn't download: allow downloads for the site and make sure the queue isn't empty.
