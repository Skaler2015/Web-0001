💡 Optimization Tips

📏
50–60 Characters
Keep titles between 50 and 60 characters. Under 50 may be too vague; over 60 gets truncated.
🔑
Keyword First
Put your primary keyword at the beginning of the title — Google weighs words earlier in the title more heavily.
🏷️
Brand at End
Add your brand name at the end separated by | or — to build brand recognition without eating into keyword space.
🎯
Match Intent
Your title must match what searchers expect to find. If Google rewrites your title, it means there's a mismatch with content.
🚫
No Keyword Stuffing
Don't repeat the same keyword multiple times. Google may filter your title or rewrite it, reducing CTR.
🔢
Use Numbers
Titles with numbers (e.g. "Top 10", "2025 Guide") consistently get higher click-through rates in search results.

🔤 What is a Meta Title?

A meta title (also called the title tag) is the HTML element that defines the title of a webpage. It appears as the clickable blue headline in Google Search results, as the browser tab label, and as the default title when the page is bookmarked. Along with the meta description, it's the single most influential on-page SEO element — both for ranking and for click-through rate.

Google typically displays titles up to about 580 pixels wide on desktop (roughly 50–60 characters in a standard font). Titles wider than this get truncated with an ellipsis (…), which can cut off important keywords and hurt CTR. On mobile, the limit is slightly narrower, around 55 characters. This checker calculates both the character count AND the estimated pixel width so you can optimize for both devices.

Character vs Pixel Width

Characters are easy to count but not the whole story — the letter "W" is much wider than "i" in Google's SERP font (Arial). A 58-character title with many wide letters might still get truncated, while a 63-character title using narrow letters might fit perfectly. This tool measures pixel width using the same font metrics Google uses, giving you a more accurate prediction.

Best Practices

  1. Target 50–60 characters for desktop safety
  2. Stay under 580 px for pixel-accurate safety
  3. Put your primary keyword first
  4. Include your brand name at the end
  5. Write for humans, not bots — CTR matters
  6. Use numbers and power words (Best, Free, Guide, 2025)
  7. Make every title unique across all pages
