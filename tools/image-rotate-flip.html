ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Image Rotate & Flip tool is a professional, privacy-first utility for correcting and changing the orientation of your photos directly in the browser. Orientation problems are among the most common issues in everyday imaging: a phone held sideways produces a rotated photo, a scanned document comes in upside-down, a selfie needs mirroring, and a landscape shot ends up with a crooked horizon. Fixing these usually means opening heavyweight editing software or uploading a private image to some website just to turn it a quarter-turn. This tool removes all of that friction. It gives you fast, precise rotation and flipping — including rotation by any custom angle — with no installation, no signup, and, crucially, no upload. Your image is loaded into the page, transformed locally on an HTML canvas, and handed straight back to you.

The core actions are immediate. One click rotates the image 90° left or 90° right, and quick buttons jump straight to 180° and 270° — exactly what you need to right a sideways photo or flip a document that scanned in the wrong direction. For orientation fixes that are not a clean quarter-turn, the tool offers custom-angle rotation: a slider and an exact numeric input let you rotate by any degree from 0 to 359, which is the correct way to straighten a tilted horizon or level a crooked scan. Crucially, when you rotate by an arbitrary angle the tool automatically expands the canvas to the new bounding box, so the corners of your image are never clipped off — a common failing of naive rotate tools that keep the original frame.

Because an angled rotation leaves triangular gaps in the corners, you control what fills them with the background setting. Choose transparent to keep those corners see-through (ideal when exporting a PNG to layer over another design), or fill them with white, black or any custom colour to match your document or layout. The live preview renders on a checkerboard so transparency is always obvious, and because the export honours your choice, what you see is exactly what you download. This level of control makes the tool useful not just for casual fixes but for real design and print work where a clean, correctly-filled rotation matters.

Flipping is equally simple and combinable. A horizontal flip mirrors the image left-to-right — perfect for selfies, which cameras often reverse, or for matching the direction of a subject — while a vertical flip mirrors top-to-bottom. Flips stack cleanly with rotation, so any of the eight distinct orientations of an image is reachable, and mirror effects are a click away. To keep experimentation safe, the tool includes proper undo, redo and reset: you can step back through every transformation, step forward again, or jump straight back to the untouched original at any time, so nothing you try is ever permanent until you download.

Output is flexible and lossless where it counts. Every transformation is applied to the image's full native resolution, not the on-screen preview, so there is no quality-reducing downscale — a rotated 4000-pixel photo stays a 4000-pixel photo. You can export as PNG (lossless, and the only sensible choice when you want transparent corners), JPG (small files ideal for the web and email) or WebP (the best size-to-quality balance in modern browsers), and a quality slider controls compression for JPG and WebP. An image-information panel keeps you oriented, reporting the current width and height, the aspect ratio, whether the image is landscape or portrait, and the file size, so you always know exactly what you are producing.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online rotate tools that send your photo to a server to process it, this tool does everything locally. Your image is read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, rotated or flipped and exported back to you — it never leaves your device, there is no account, and nothing is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for personal photos, confidential scans and ID documents alike, and it means the tool keeps working offline once the page has loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards too, with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines, high-contrast support and a layout that adapts from a spacious desktop workspace to a single, touch-friendly column on phones. In short, it is a fast, precise, genuinely private orientation studio that lives entirely in your browser — capable enough for design work, simple enough for a quick quarter-turn.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Angled rotation has black/white corners: that's the background — set it to Transparent and export as PNG.
  • Corners get cut off: they shouldn't — the canvas auto-expands; make sure you're not viewing a cropped older result.
  • Transparent corners turned black in JPG: JPG has no transparency — export as PNG or pick a background colour.
  • Image looks lower resolution: it isn't — rotation uses full native pixels; the preview is just scaled to fit.
  • Custom angle field won't accept a value: enter a whole number from 0 to 359.
  • WebP export unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG or JPG.
  • Flip and rotate look confusing together: use Reset to start over, then apply one change at a time.
