ℹ️ About This Tool

The Professional Bulk Certificate & ID Card Generator is a free, browser-based application for creating personalised documents at scale. It replaces the slow, error-prone routine of editing a template again and again for every recipient with a clean two-part workflow: design the layout once, then merge a spreadsheet so that every row produces its own finished certificate or card. Whether you need ten certificates for a workshop or five thousand ID cards for a university intake, the same simple process applies, and every document is produced right on your computer without ever uploading a name, photo or mark to a server.

Everything starts with a design. You can upload your own artwork as a JPG, PNG, WebP or SVG — a certificate frame from your designer, a branded ID card back, an award border — or begin from the built-in template library, which includes school, college, internship, experience, participation, appreciation and achievement certificates alongside student ID, employee ID and event pass layouts. Once a design is on the canvas, you build up the document by adding fields. A field can be plain text (a name, a course, a date), a QR code, a barcode, a photo, or an uploaded signature or stamp. You drag each field to exactly where it belongs, resize and rotate it, and the editor helps you with snap-to-grid alignment and smart centre guides so everything lines up cleanly. Undo and redo, duplicate, lock and hide give you the same control you would expect from a desktop design tool.

Text fields are where most of the personalisation lives, so they support rich typography. You choose the font family, size, weight and style, and fine-tune letter spacing, line height, colour and opacity. For designs that sit on busy backgrounds you can add a drop shadow or an outline stroke so the text stays legible. Every field can either hold a fixed value — useful for a shared line like "Issued by the Principal" — or be bound to a column in your data file, in which case it is filled in individually for each record. The tool ships with a long list of ready-made field names that cover the vast majority of Indian and international documents: Name, Father's Name, Mother's Name, Roll Number, Registration Number, Certificate Number, Student ID, Employee ID, Department, Designation, Course Name, Marks, Grade, Percentage, College, University, Institute, Date, Expiry Date, Issued By and more, and you can always add your own custom fields.

Two field types deserve special mention because they make documents verifiable. The QR code field turns any column — a verification URL, a unique certificate number, a student record link — into a scannable code, so a recipient or verifier can confirm authenticity with a phone. The barcode field supports the common retail and access formats Code128, Code39, EAN13 and UPC, which is exactly what event scanners and library or HR systems expect. Photos are handled just as flexibly: point a photo field at a column of image URLs and each card gets the right person's picture, cropped as a rectangle, rounded rectangle or circle, with an optional coloured border. A separate signature or stamp field lets you drop a fixed image — a scanned signature or an official seal — onto every document.

The data side is designed to be forgiving. You can import a CSV, a tab-separated TSV, or a real Excel XLSX workbook. Headers are detected automatically, and a single click auto-maps them to matching fields by name. The imported records appear in a searchable table so you can spot problems quickly, and the built-in validation highlights the issues that most often ruin a batch: duplicate certificate numbers, empty required fields, invalid dates and malformed email addresses. Fixing these before you generate saves reprinting later. When you are ready, you pick an export format and range. Small runs are happiest as a single multi-page PDF; large runs or "one file per person" needs are better served by a ZIP of PNG or JPG images named after a field you choose. You can also export just the current preview record as a PNG, JPG or SVG. Print controls let you place the design on A4, A5 or Letter paper in portrait or landscape and add crop marks for professional trimming.

Because generation happens locally, it is both private and fast. The engine renders documents in small asynchronous batches with a progress bar, so even a run of several thousand records will not freeze the tab, and nothing is ever transmitted. That local-first architecture also means the tool keeps working offline once the page has loaded, which is invaluable during an event or in a school computer lab with unreliable internet. Combined with keyboard shortcuts, a responsive layout that adapts from desktop to phone, and accessible, screen-reader-friendly controls, the result is a serious production tool that anyone on your team can pick up without training — powerful enough for a registrar's office, simple enough for a class teacher, and trustworthy enough for confidential HR records.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • My CSV columns are wrong or merged: ensure the file has a single header row and is comma-separated (or use TSV/XLSX). Re-export from Excel as "CSV UTF-8".
  • Photos are blank on export: the image URLs are cross-origin without CORS and taint the canvas. Host the photos where CORS is allowed, or download and place a signature/stamp image locally.
  • A barcode won't render: EAN13 needs 12–13 digits and UPC needs 11–12 digits; switch to Code128 for arbitrary text.
  • Text is cut off or overlaps: reduce the font size, widen the field, or enable line height/word wrapping by adding line breaks in your data.
  • A large batch is slow: export as a ZIP of images or use the First 100/1000 range, and reduce the export quality slider.
  • Fonts look different when printed: stick to common web-safe fonts, or convert your design to an image template that already contains the styled text.
