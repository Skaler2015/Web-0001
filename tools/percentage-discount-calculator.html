    ℹ️ About This Tool

    The Percentage Calculator is a fast, private, browser-based tool that bundles more than thirty percentage-based calculations into a single clean dashboard — and, crucially, shows the formula and a step-by-step solution for every one of them. Percentages appear everywhere: a discount at a shop, marks in an exam, interest on savings, a salary hike, GST on a bill, profit margin on a product, year-over-year growth in a report. But the arithmetic behind each of these questions is subtly different, and it is easy to reach for the wrong formula. Instead of hunting for a separate calculator for each situation, this tool lets you pick the exact calculation you need from organised categories and get the answer instantly, with the working shown so you can learn it, teach it, or double-check it.

    The calculators are grouped into six categories. Basic covers the everyday building blocks — X% of Y, "X is what percent of Y", increasing or decreasing a number by a percentage, the percentage change and percentage difference between two values, reverse percentage (finding the original number when you know a part and its percentage), and adding or subtracting a percentage. Business handles commerce — profit and loss percentage, profit margin, markup, commission, and sales tax, GST and VAT (both adding tax to a base and reading it off a total). Finance covers interest and loan percentages, salary increase and reduction, return on investment (ROI) and compound annual growth rate (CAGR). Education gives you marks, attendance and grade percentages. Conversions turns percentages into decimals, fractions and ratios and back again. And Advanced adds weighted percentage, percentage error, percentage split and percentage allocation for more specialised needs.

    What sets this calculator apart is transparency. Every result appears in a large, easy-to-read card, but underneath it you also get the exact formula used and a numbered step-by-step breakdown that walks through the calculation with your actual numbers substituted in. For a discount, for example, it does not just tell you the final price — it shows the discount amount, the formula Final = Price − (Price × Rate ÷ 100), and each arithmetic step. This makes the tool genuinely useful for students learning the method, teachers preparing examples, and professionals who need to justify a figure rather than just quote it. Summary cards highlight the key sub-results (such as the discount amount and the amount saved, or the profit and the margin), so the numbers that matter are never buried.

    The tool is built for real-world money and study work, so it includes the practical touches that matter. A currency selector supports the rupee, dollar, euro, pound and dirham for the money-based calculators. A number-format option switches between the Indian system (with lakh and crore grouping, e.g. 1,00,000) and the international system (100,000), and you can choose how many decimal places to show. Robust validation guards against empty fields, negative numbers where they do not make sense, very large numbers, and — importantly — division by zero, which is the classic trap in percentage change and "what percent" calculations; instead of showing a broken result, the tool explains the problem.

    Every calculation can be copied (the result or the full steps), shared through your device's native share sheet, printed, and exported as a PDF, PNG image, CSV or Excel spreadsheet, or structured JSON. A local history keeps your recent calculations in your browser so you can revisit and reuse them, download the whole log, or clear it in one click. Because everything runs as JavaScript on your own device, the calculator is lightweight and fast, works offline once the page has loaded, and is completely private — none of your numbers are ever uploaded to a server, there is no sign-up, and nothing is tracked against you.

    Who is it for? Students checking exam scores and attendance; teachers building worked examples; shoppers comparing which of two sale offers actually saves more; shopkeepers and small-business owners working out markups, margins, commissions and GST; accountants and finance professionals computing ROI, CAGR and tax percentages; and anyone who wants a quick, reliable percentage answer with the working shown. Whether you need a five-second discount check or a fully explained business calculation, this tool puts every common percentage question in one place and shows you exactly how the answer was reached.

    📐 Key Formulas

    • X% of Y: (X ÷ 100) × Y
    • X is what % of Y: (X ÷ Y) × 100
    • Percentage change: ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100
    • Percentage difference: (|A − B| ÷ ((A + B) ÷ 2)) × 100
    • Reverse percentage: Original = Part ÷ (Percent ÷ 100)
    • Discount: Save = Price × Rate ÷ 100; Final = Price − Save
    • Profit %: ((Sell − Cost) ÷ Cost) × 100; Margin %: ((Sell − Cost) ÷ Sell) × 100; Markup %: ((Sell − Cost) ÷ Cost) × 100
    • Tax / GST / VAT (add): Tax = Amount × Rate ÷ 100; Total = Amount + Tax
    • ROI: ((Final − Initial) ÷ Initial) × 100; CAGR: ((End ÷ Begin)^(1 ÷ Years) − 1) × 100
    • Marks / attendance %: (Obtained ÷ Total) × 100
    • Weighted %: Σ(value × weight) ÷ Σweight
    • Percentage error: (|Measured − Exact| ÷ |Exact|) × 100

    🛠️ Troubleshooting

    • Result shows "—" or a divide-by-zero note: A denominator is zero (e.g., "what percent of 0" or change from 0). Enter a non-zero base value.
    • Answer looks wrong: Check you picked the right calculator — percentage change (new vs old) differs from percentage difference (symmetric), and margin differs from markup.
    • Number grouping looks odd: Switch between Indian and International formatting in Options; Indian uses lakh/crore grouping.
    • Too many/few decimals: Change the decimal-places option (0, 2, 4 or 6).
    • Currency symbol not needed: Percentage, education and conversion calculators ignore currency; it only affects money calculators.
    • PDF/PNG export not working: These use a library loaded from the internet. Offline, use Print or CSV/Excel/JSON, which always work.
    • History disappeared: History lives only in your browser; clearing browser data or using private mode will not retain it.
