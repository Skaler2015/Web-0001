See exactly how strong a password is, and what's missing, before you start using it.

ℹ️ This check happens entirely in your browser — your password is never sent anywhere or stored.

ℹ️ About This Tool

Not all passwords that "feel" secure actually are — a word with a couple of numbers swapped in for letters, or a familiar phrase with the first letter capitalized, often looks complex at a glance but is exactly the kind of pattern that password-cracking tools are specifically built to try first. This checker evaluates a password against the actual factors that determine real-world strength: length, the variety of character types used, and whether it avoids common predictable patterns, then gives a clear strength rating along with a breakdown of exactly what's helping or hurting.

Type any password into the box and the strength meter, rating, and detailed checklist update instantly as you type, so you can see in real time how adding a number, a symbol, or a few extra characters changes the overall strength. This makes it easy to tweak a password until it's genuinely strong rather than just guessing.

How to use:

  1. Type a password into the box (click the eye icon to show or hide it).
  2. Watch the strength meter and checklist update live.
  3. Adjust the password until every check passes and the rating reaches "Strong."

What actually makes a password strong

Length matters more than almost anything else — a longer password is exponentially harder to crack by brute force than a shorter one, even if the shorter one has more symbols. A genuinely strong password combines length (at least 12 characters, ideally more) with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols, while avoiding dictionary words, names, keyboard patterns like "qwerty," and sequences like "1234." A long, random passphrase made of several unrelated words is often both easier to remember and harder to crack than a short, complex-looking jumble.

It's worth using a tool like this before settling on a password for anything that matters — email, banking, or any account tied to your identity — since the checks here mirror the same kind of rules many security-conscious websites enforce behind the scenes anyway. Reusing the same password across multiple sites is a separate but equally important risk this tool doesn't measure: even a technically strong password becomes a liability if it's reused everywhere and one of those sites is ever breached. Pairing a genuinely strong, unique password for each important account with a password manager to keep track of them is the most reliable approach for staying secure without having to memorize dozens of complex strings.

असल में password को strong क्या बनाता है

Length लगभग किसी भी और चीज़ से ज़्यादा मायने रखती है — एक लंबा password brute force से तोड़ना exponentially मुश्किल होता है किसी छोटे password से, भले ही छोटे में ज़्यादा symbols हों। एक genuinely strong password length (कम से कम 12 characters, ज़्यादा बेहतर) को uppercase, lowercase, numbers, और symbols के मिश्रण के साथ जोड़ता है, साथ ही dictionary words, names, "qwerty" जैसे keyboard patterns, और "1234" जैसी sequences से बचता है। कई असंबंधित words से बना एक लंबा, random passphrase अक्सर याद रखने में आसान और तोड़ने में मुश्किल दोनों होता है, किसी छोटे, complex दिखने वाले मिश्रण से ज़्यादा।
