    ℹ️ About This Tool

    The GST Calculator is a fast, private, browser-based utility that works out Goods and Services Tax the way Indian businesses, freelancers, shopkeepers, accountants and everyday buyers actually need it. GST is the unified indirect tax that replaced the older tangle of VAT, service tax, excise duty and other levies, and it touches almost every sale of goods and services in the country. On paper the maths looks simple — multiply by a percentage — but in practice you are constantly switching directions: sometimes you have a base price and need the tax-inclusive total, sometimes you have a printed bill and need to work backwards to the taxable value, and often you need that tax split correctly into CGST, SGST or IGST. This tool handles every one of those situations instantly, with no spreadsheets, no logins and no data ever leaving your device.

    There are two core modes. In Add GST (exclusive) mode you enter a base price and the applicable rate, and the calculator shows the GST amount and the final price your customer pays. In Remove GST (inclusive / reverse) mode you start from a GST-inclusive figure — the round number usually printed on a retail bill — and the tool reverse-engineers the original taxable value and exactly how much of that total was tax. Reverse GST is a lifesaver at return-filing time, because invoices are commonly shown as tax-inclusive round figures while your GSTR returns require the taxable value and the tax broken out separately. Getting that division wrong by even a rupee, repeated across dozens of line items, quietly distorts your books; letting the calculator do it removes that whole class of error.

    Beyond the basic add and remove operations, this is a full commercial-grade GST engine. You can choose the supply type: an intra-state supply (buyer and seller in the same state) splits the tax equally into Central GST (CGST) and State GST (SGST), while an inter-state supply (across state lines, or imports) applies a single Integrated GST (IGST). The calculator makes that split automatically — for an 18% intra-state supply you instantly see 9% CGST and 9% SGST, and for the same rate inter-state you see 18% IGST. It supports every current slab (0%, 0.25%, 3%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% and the 40% special rate) plus a custom rate field for anything unusual, and an optional compensation cess that applies on top of GST for demerit and luxury goods such as tobacco, aerated drinks and certain vehicles.

    Real invoices are rarely just "price times rate", so the tool also models the messy parts. You can apply a discount — either a flat amount or a percentage — before GST is charged, exactly as trade discounts are treated under GST law. You can add extra charges such as shipping, freight, handling, packing and insurance, which are added to the taxable value so tax is computed on the composite supply the way the GST rules generally require. An automatic round-off option nudges the final payable to the nearest whole unit (and shows you the tiny round-off adjustment separately, just like a professional invoice), or you can switch it off to keep full decimals. A currency selector supports INR, USD, EUR, GBP, AED, AUD and CAD for exporters and cross-border sellers, and the grand total is automatically converted into words using the Indian numbering system (lakh and crore) for rupees, so you can drop it straight onto an invoice.

    Every result is transparent, not a black box. A live formula panel shows the exact equation being used for your current mode and supply type, and a numbered step-by-step breakdown walks through each stage — taxable value, discount, charges, GST, cess, round-off and total — using your actual numbers. This makes the tool genuinely useful for learning and for double-checking a figure someone else has quoted you. One-click copy buttons put the GST amount, the total, or a full plain-text summary onto your clipboard, and a full export panel saves your calculation as a PDF, a PNG image, a CSV or Excel spreadsheet, or a structured JSON file for your records or accounting software.

    The tool also remembers your work. A local history log stores your recent calculations in your browser so you can revisit, compare and reuse them, and you can download the whole history as CSV or JSON or clear it in one click. A built-in GST slab reference table and common HSN/SAC pointers help you sanity-check which rate to use, and a set of worked examples shows the calculator in action. Because everything runs in JavaScript on your own machine, the tool is fast, works offline once loaded, and is completely private — your amounts, clients and numbers are never uploaded to any server, there is no sign-up, and nothing is tracked against you.

    Who is it for? Retailers and wholesalers pricing goods; freelancers and consultants raising service invoices; accountants and tax practitioners reconciling books; e-commerce sellers computing tax across states; students and new business owners learning how CGST, SGST and IGST fit together; and anyone who has ever squinted at a bill wondering how much of it was tax. Whether you need a five-second answer or a fully broken-down, exportable GST computation, this calculator is designed to be accurate, clear and effortless.

    📚 GST Slabs & Rates (India)

    A quick reference to India's main GST rates. Always confirm the exact rate and HSN/SAC for your product or service on the official GST portal, as the GST Council revises these from time to time.

    RateTypical items (illustrative)
    0%Fresh fruits & vegetables, unbranded food grains, milk, salt, printed books, most essential exempt items.
    0.25%Rough diamonds and certain precious/semi-precious stones.
    3%Gold, silver and gold jewellery / precious metals.
    5%Packaged essentials, footwear & apparel under thresholds, economy transport, many daily-use goods.
    12%Processed foods, some apparel, business-class travel, select electronics & utility items.
    18%Most goods and the majority of services — IT, telecom, restaurants (non-AC/standard), many appliances.
    28%Luxury & sin goods — high-end cars, tobacco, aerated drinks, premium consumer durables.
    40%Special higher rate applied to specific notified categories.
    + CessCompensation cess is charged in addition to GST on demerit/luxury goods (e.g. tobacco, some vehicles).

    HSN/SAC note: Goods are classified by HSN codes and services by SAC codes; the code determines the correct rate. When in doubt, look up your HSN/SAC on the GST portal or ask your tax advisor rather than guessing the slab.

    ➗ Formulas used

    • Add GST: GST = Taxable × Rate ÷ 100; Total = Taxable + GST + Cess
    • Remove GST: Taxable = Total ÷ (1 + (Rate + Cess) ÷ 100); GST = Taxable × Rate ÷ 100
    • Intra-state split: CGST = SGST = GST ÷ 2
    • Inter-state: IGST = GST (full amount)
    • Taxable value (Add mode): (Base − Discount) + Extra charges

    🧮 Worked examples

    1. Add 18% intra-state on ₹1,000: GST = ₹180 → CGST ₹90 + SGST ₹90; Total = ₹1,180.
    2. Remove 18% from a ₹1,180 bill: Taxable = 1180 ÷ 1.18 = ₹1,000; GST = ₹180.
    3. Add 12% inter-state on ₹5,000 with ₹200 freight: Taxable = ₹5,200; IGST = ₹624; Total = ₹5,824.
    4. Add 28% + 12% cess on ₹10,000: GST = ₹2,800; Cess = ₹1,200; Total = ₹14,000.

    🛠️ Troubleshooting

    • Result stays ₹0.00: Make sure you have entered an amount and selected a non-zero rate. A 0% slab correctly produces ₹0 GST.
    • "Enter a valid amount" message: The amount must be a positive number below the maximum limit. Remove any letters or symbols and avoid negative values.
    • Custom rate ignored: Typing in the custom-rate box clears the slab buttons and uses your custom value. Clear the box to return to the slab buttons.
    • PDF/PNG export not working: These use an export library loaded from the internet. If you are fully offline, use Print (which works offline) or export to CSV/Excel/JSON, which are always available.
    • Amount in words looks off for foreign currency: Words use the Indian numbering system (lakh/crore) for INR and the international system (thousand/million) for other currencies.
    • History disappeared: History is stored in your browser. Clearing browser data, using private/incognito mode, or switching devices/browsers will not carry it over.
    • Numbers don't match my invoice: Check whether charges should be taxed, whether the discount is pre- or post-GST, and whether round-off is on — small policy differences change the total.
