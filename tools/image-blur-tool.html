ℹ️ About This Tool

A blur effect softens the detail in a photo by smoothing out sharp edges, which is useful for a surprising number of everyday tasks: blurring a busy background so a product or a person stands out more clearly, softening a photo for a dreamy or artistic look, creating a blurred placeholder image while a real photo loads on a website, or roughly obscuring a section of an image for privacy without removing it entirely. Doing this in a full photo editor usually means learning layers, masks, and filter menus — this tool reduces it to a single slider.

Upload any photo and drag the blur strength slider — the preview updates instantly as you move it, so you can dial in exactly how much softening looks right before downloading. The blur is applied evenly across the whole image; if you only need to blur one specific area like a face or a license plate rather than the entire photo, the dedicated Image Pixelator tool lets you draw a box over just that region instead.

How to use:

  1. Upload a photo.
  2. Drag the blur strength slider until it looks right in the live preview.
  3. Click Download to save the blurred image.

Blur vs pixelation

Blur and pixelation both obscure detail, but they look and behave differently. Blur smooths colors together into soft gradients, which keeps a natural, photographic feel and works well for backgrounds, depth-of-field effects, or artistic softening. Pixelation instead replaces detail with visible flat-colored blocks, giving the unmistakable "censored" look used to hide faces or sensitive text — that effect cannot be reversed by simply un-blurring it, which is why pixelation is the safer choice specifically when the goal is hiding identifying information rather than just softening an image.

A common real-world use is taking a sharp photo of a product or a person and blurring just the background behind them using a separate cropped copy, then layering the two back together in a different editor — this tool handles the blurring half of that workflow cleanly with a single slider, leaving you free to focus on the composition rather than fighting with filter settings. The effect works on any photo format you can upload, and the final download is always a clean PNG file regardless of what you started with, so there's no need to worry about format compatibility before or after using this tool.
