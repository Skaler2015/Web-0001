📊 What is Schema Markup?

Schema markup (structured data) is code you add to your HTML that helps search engines understand your content better. It enables rich results in Google — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, event dates, price information, and more directly in search results — increasing visibility and CTR dramatically.

JSON-LD (the format this tool generates) is the recommended format by Google. You simply paste the generated code inside your page's <head> section. No HTML modification needed — it's completely separate from your visible content.

Schema Types Explained

  1. FAQ — Accordion-style questions appear directly in Google
  2. Article — Author, date and publisher in search results
  3. Product — Price, ratings and availability in results
  4. LocalBusiness — Address, hours, phone in knowledge panel
  5. BreadcrumbList — Navigation trail shown below title
  6. Event — Date, venue and ticket info in results
