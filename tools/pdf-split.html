ℹ️ About PDF Split Tool

The PDF Split tool by ApneSoftware allows you to extract specific pages from any PDF document and save them as a separate file — completely free, without uploading your files to any server, and without requiring any account or registration. Whether you need to extract a single chapter from a large report, pull out specific invoices from a combined statement, or separate pages from a scanned document, this tool handles it in seconds.

Simply upload your PDF, choose your preferred split method, preview pages to confirm the content, and download the result. Everything runs locally in your browser using modern web technology, which means your files remain completely private throughout the process.

Multiple Split Methods

The tool offers four ways to split your PDF: Page Range lets you specify ranges like 1-5 or 6-10, making it easy to extract sequential sections. Selected Pages lets you click individual thumbnails to pick exactly which pages you want, ideal for non-sequential extractions. Every Page splits the entire document into individual single-page PDFs. Custom Groups lets you define multiple output files in one step using the pipe separator, so you can produce several different extractions simultaneously.

How to Split a PDF

Upload your PDF → choose a split method → select pages or enter a range → preview to confirm → click Split PDF → download your extracted PDF file. The preview feature lets you visually confirm each page's content before committing to the split, preventing errors when working with unfamiliar documents.

Why Use This Tool

Unlike online services that require uploading your documents to a remote server, this tool processes everything locally. This is especially important for sensitive documents like legal contracts, financial statements, medical records, or personal identification pages, where privacy and data security are critical concerns. There are no file size limits imposed by server quotas, no compression artifacts from server-side processing, and no risk of your files being retained after the session ends.

PDF Split Use Cases

PDF splitting is useful in a wide range of professional and personal situations. Students extract relevant chapters from large textbook PDFs for easier reading. Legal professionals separate specific exhibits or pages from lengthy case files. Accountants pull individual monthly statements from annual combined PDF exports. Teachers extract specific exercises or worksheets from comprehensive resource books. HR teams separate individual employee documents from bulk-generated PDFs. Photographers and designers extract specific portfolio pages. In all these cases, the ability to quickly and precisely extract exactly the pages needed — without any additional software installation — makes this browser-based tool genuinely useful.
