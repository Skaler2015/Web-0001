ℹ️ About This Tool

PDFs often have clickable hyperlinks tucked inside them — references in a research paper, source links in a report, contact pages in a brochure, or resource links in a slide deck exported to PDF — but there's no built-in way to see them all listed at once. Finding them means clicking through every page individually and hoping you don't miss one, which gets tedious fast in a long document.

This tool scans every page of a PDF and pulls out every embedded hyperlink it finds, listing the destination URL along with which page it appears on. The result is a simple, scannable list you can review, copy, or download all at once, rather than hunting through pages one at a time.

How to use:

  1. Upload a PDF.
  2. Every link found is listed below, along with its page number.
  3. Click any link to open it directly, or copy/download the full list.

What counts as a link here

This tool finds clickable link annotations embedded in the PDF — the kind you'd normally click directly in a PDF reader, often added when a document is exported from Word, PowerPoint, or a webpage. It does not scan for plain text that merely looks like a URL but isn't actually a clickable link (for example, a website address typed as plain text in a scanned document) — those would need to be read visually or extracted with a text-extraction tool instead, since they aren't stored as links inside the PDF itself.

This is particularly useful for checking a document before distributing it widely — a report or proposal with broken or outdated links looks unprofessional, and manually clicking through dozens of pages to verify every reference is impractical. Researchers reviewing a paper's citations, editors checking a brochure before it goes to print, or anyone repurposing content from an old PDF who needs to recover its source links can use this to get a complete inventory in seconds rather than working through the document page by page. Since everything runs locally in the browser, even a confidential or unpublished document can be checked without it ever being uploaded anywhere, which matters for legal documents, internal reports, or drafts that haven't been cleared for wider circulation yet, and it gives a quick way to confirm there are no stray, outdated, or broken-looking links left behind before a document is sent out or published.

यहां "link" का मतलब क्या है
