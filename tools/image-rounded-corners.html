ℹ️ About This Tool

The ApneSoftware Rounded Corners & Circle Crop tool is a professional, privacy-first utility for reshaping images directly in your browser. Rectangular photos are everywhere, but modern design rarely wants a hard-edged rectangle: profile pictures are round, app icons are squircles, UI cards have softly rounded corners, and social stickers come in stars and hearts. Achieving these shapes normally means firing up heavyweight design software or uploading a private photo to some website just to round a corner. This tool removes that friction entirely. It lets you round corners, crop to a circle, or cut an image into any of a dozen shapes — with backgrounds, borders and shadows — and it does all of it locally, with no installation, no signup and no upload. Your image is loaded into the page, shaped on an HTML canvas, and handed straight back to you as a clean, transparent PNG.

The heart of the tool is its shape engine. The two most common needs are covered first and best: rounded corners, with a radius you control precisely via a slider (in pixels or as a percentage of the image, so the same setting scales across different sizes), and a circle crop that turns any photo into a round avatar. Beyond those, the tool offers a full palette of shapes — oval, capsule (a pill), squircle (the smooth, iOS-style rounded square), hexagon, diamond, triangle, star and heart — each rendered with proper anti-aliased clipping so the edges are crisp, not jagged. Whatever shape you pick, the area outside it becomes transparent by default, so the result sits cleanly on any background.

Shaping is only half the job; styling is the other half. The background control lets you keep the outside transparent or fill it with white, black, a custom solid colour, or a two-colour gradient — useful when you want the shaped image to sit on a coloured card rather than float. A border can be added around the shape in any colour and width, and drawn solid, dashed or dotted, which is perfect for framing avatars or giving stickers a clean outline. A drop shadow with adjustable blur, offset, colour and opacity lifts the shape off the page for a modern, layered look. Because these effects extend beyond the image's edges, the tool automatically enlarges the output canvas to fit them, so a shadow or thick border is never clipped.

To make experimenting effortless, everything updates in a live preview rendered on a checkerboard background, so you can always see exactly which areas are transparent. The tool includes proper undo, redo and reset, so you can try a shape, add a shadow, change your mind, and step back without starting over — nothing is committed until you download. An image-information panel keeps you oriented with the current dimensions, aspect ratio, orientation and file size, so you always know what you are producing.

Output is flexible and high quality. All shaping is applied to the image's full native resolution, not the on-screen preview, so there is no quality-losing downscale. The default export is a transparent PNG — the correct format for rounded corners, circles and any shape with see-through areas — but you can also export WebP (smaller, still supports transparency) or JPG (smallest, though transparency becomes a background fill), with a quality slider controlling compression. The download is immediate and sensibly named, ready to use as an avatar, an app asset, a sticker or a card image.

Privacy is fundamental. Unlike many online image editors that send your photo to a server, this tool does everything locally. Your image is read into the browser, drawn onto a canvas, shaped and exported back to you — it never leaves your device, there is no account, and nothing is tracked or stored. That makes it safe for personal photos, brand assets and client work alike, and it means the tool keeps working offline once the page has loaded. The interface follows modern accessibility standards too, with keyboard-operable controls, ARIA labelling, visible focus outlines, high-contrast support and a layout that adapts from a spacious desktop workspace to a single, touch-friendly column on phones. In short, it is a fast, versatile, genuinely private image-shaping studio that lives entirely in your browser — powerful enough for design work, simple enough for a quick round-corner or circle crop.

⭕ Rounded Corner Guide

  • Small radius (a few %): a subtle softening — good for photos and banners.
  • Medium radius (10–25%): the modern card look used across apps and websites.
  • Large radius (near 50%): approaches a capsule; on a square it becomes a circle.
  • Percentage mode keeps the same look across different image sizes, unlike a fixed pixel radius.
  • Always export as PNG so the rounded corners stay transparent.

🔵 Circle Crop Guide

  • Circle crop uses the largest circle that fits your image, centred in the frame.
  • For the best avatar, start from a square-ish photo so nothing important is cut off.
  • Add a border for a clean ring around the avatar, and a shadow to lift it off the page.
  • Keep the background transparent and export PNG for a drop-in profile picture.
  • Use a gradient or solid background if you want a coloured badge instead.

🛠️ Troubleshooting

  • Corners aren't transparent: export as PNG or WebP — JPG can't store transparency.
  • Circle looks like an oval: that's expected on a non-square image — the circle fits the shorter side; crop to a square first for a full circle.
  • Shadow or border is cut off: it shouldn't be — the canvas auto-expands; make sure you're viewing the latest render.
  • Radius does nothing on circle/star: the radius slider only applies to the Rounded shape.
  • JPG has a coloured box instead of transparency: JPG has no alpha — choose PNG, or set a matching background.
  • WebP export unavailable: some older browsers can't encode WebP — choose PNG.
  • Result looks lower resolution: it isn't — shaping uses full native pixels; the preview is just scaled to fit.
